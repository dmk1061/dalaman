Saklıkent Gorge — An Icy Adventure in the "Hidden City"
Saklıkent Gorge, whose name translates as "Hidden City", is one of the longest (18 km) and deepest (up to 300 m) gorges in Turkey. It is a true kingdom of water and stone, where you can hide from the scorching summer heat and get a dose of adrenaline. A visit to Saklıkent is not just sightseeing, but an active adventure.

Main Feature: A Walk Along the Icy River
The main attraction of Saklıkent is a hike deep into the gorge right along the bed of the icy mountain river Eşen.

First trial: To get to the main part of the canyon, you need to cross a turbulent and very cold stream that bursts out from under the cliff. The water here is icy, and the current is strong. For safety, a rope is stretched across the stream. This first step is the most invigorating and memorable moment.

The path deep inside: After crossing the stream, you enter a narrow canyon where you continue your journey, walking through the water (depth varies from ankle to knee), climbing over boulders, and admiring the bizarre shapes of rocks polished by water over thousands of years.

Atmosphere and Infrastructure
Entrance zone: In front of the entrance to the canyon itself (entrance is paid, as it is a national park), there is a large tourist area. There are many restaurants with unique gazebos built on platforms directly above the river. This is an excellent place to have lunch with fresh trout after the hike.

What to wear: You definitely need special shoes — rubber "water" shoes with a heel strap. You can bring them with you or rent/buy them on site. Ordinary flip-flops will be washed away by the current. Clothes should be something you do not mind getting wet.

Other entertainment: In addition to hiking, rafting (tubing down a calmer part of the river) and ziplining are very popular in the Saklıkent area.

Pros and Cons
Pros:

A unique and exciting adventure.

Ideal escape from the summer heat (it is always cool in the gorge).

Incredibly beautiful and photogenic landscapes.

Opportunity to combine with other activities (rafting, ziplining).

Cons:

Requires a certain level of physical fitness and is not suitable for people with mobility issues.

Very slippery stones and icy water.

It can be very crowded during the peak season.

Not suitable for very young children.

Who is Saklıkent Gorge suitable for?
Saklıkent is a place for lovers of active recreation and adventure. It is an ideal day trip for those who are bored with beach holidays and want something new and exciting. It will appeal to both young people and families with teenage children. This is a wonderful opportunity to challenge the elements, cool off on a hot day, and get a lot of bright emotions and impressions that will be remembered for a long time.
