# Butterfly Valley (Kelebekler Vadisi) — The Hidden Canyon and Steep Cliffs of Ölüdeniz
A scenic, deep canyon featuring a pebble beach and waterfall, accessible only by sea and famous for its population of rare Jersey Tiger moths.

Butterfly Valley is one of the most recognizable and awe-inspiring natural landmarks in Turkey. This deep canyon, wedged between sheer cliffs up to 350 meters high, opens directly to a crystal-clear turquoise sea. The entire valley is a protected nature reserve, meaning any permanent construction is strictly banned, preserving its pristine, wild appearance.

The valley owes its name to the rare Jersey Tiger moths that inhabit the area. Deeper inside the canyon, reached by a path running alongside a stream, lies a cool, refreshing waterfall. During peak summer, many butterflies can be spotted here, though the influx of visitors has made them more elusive. The beach itself is pebbly, with clear, deep water.

Access to the valley is virtually boat-only. Regular shuttle boats (boat dolmuşes) depart from nearby [Ölüdeniz](/en/fethiye), and numerous daily excursion yachts make swimming stops here. While there is a hiking trail descending from the village of Faralya at the top, it is extremely steep, dangerous, and requires climbing skills, making it highly discouraged for general tourists.

### Practical Information
- Location: 15 km south of [Fethiye](/en/fethiye) (accessible only by sea from Ölüdeniz)
- Road: Boat only; the hiking trail down from Faralya is hazardous and not recommended
- Infrastructure: Eco-campsite (wooden huts and tents), basic showers and toilets, a single rustic cafe-bar with limited options
- Entrance: Entry to the valley/waterfall area is paid (approx. 100-250 TL, collected by the cooperative for reserve maintenance); boat transfers are charged separately
- Tip: To experience the silence of this "hidden world," catch the first morning boat or stay overnight at the campsite. Between 11:00 and 16:00, the beach is very crowded with excursion tours. Sturdy shoes are required for the hike to the waterfall.
