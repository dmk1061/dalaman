Butterfly Valley — A Lost World in the Embrace of Cliffs
Butterfly Valley is a place shrouded in legends and possessing incredible energy. Squeezed between two high, almost vertical cliffs, this valley is accessible only from the sea, which has allowed it to preserve its pristine beauty and unique atmosphere. It is a 1st degree natural protected area, so permanent construction is prohibited here.

Main Features: Butterflies, Waterfall, and Complete Isolation
Butterflies: The valley got its name due to the population of the Jersey Tiger butterfly, which lives deep in the gorge. In certain periods (usually in the middle of summer), they can be seen by the thousands, covering stones and trees. However, it is important to understand that butterflies are very sensitive to noise, and their numbers vary from year to year.

Waterfall: Deep in the canyon, there is a small waterfall flowing down the rocks. The path to it is a small adventure requiring scrambling over rocks and crossing a stream. The cool water of the waterfall is a great escape from the heat.

Isolation: You can get into the valley only by boat, which creates the feeling of being on a deserted island. There are no roads, no cars, no big hotels. Only you, the sea, the cliffs, and nature.

Beach and Atmosphere
Coast: A small but very picturesque beach with white pebbles and crystal clear, turquoise water.

Atmosphere: Like in the neighboring Kabak Bay, a very relaxed, bohemian, "hippie" atmosphere reigns here. This is a place for those looking for freedom and simplicity. Here you can live in a tent or a simple wooden bungalow provided by the only local enterprise.

Infrastructure: Very basic. There is one cafe-bar where you can buy food and drinks, showers, and toilets. Accommodation in tents or bungalows is also offered.

How to Get There
By shuttle boat (dolmuş-boat): The most popular way. Shuttle boats leave regularly from Ölüdeniz.

As part of a boat tour: Almost all daily boat trips from Ölüdeniz make a stop here for swimming.

On foot (only for experienced hikers): There is a very steep and dangerous path from above, from the village of Faralya. It is not recommended for unprepared tourists.

Pros and Cons
Pros:

Incredible natural beauty and the feeling of a "lost world".

Unique atmosphere of freedom and unity with nature.

Opportunity to see butterflies and swim at the waterfall.

A great place for a "digital detox".

Cons:

Very crowded during the day due to the influx of excursion boats that dock for 1-2 hours.

The infrastructure is very simple, not for comfort lovers.

You might not see butterflies if you come out of season or if it turned out to be a "poor" year for them.

Who is Butterfly Valley suitable for?
Butterfly Valley is a place for romantics, adventurers, nature lovers, and anyone who wants to see one of the most iconic places in Turkey. To truly feel its spirit, it is best to stay here overnight in a tent or bungalow. When the last excursion boats leave in the evening, the valley is transformed, plunging into silence, broken only by the sound of the surf and the chirping of cicadas. This is an unforgettable experience that allows you to feel like you are at the end of the world.
