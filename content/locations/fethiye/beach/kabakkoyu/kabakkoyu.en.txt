# Kabak Cove (Kabak Koyu) — Secluded Bohemian Oasis and a Haven for Nature Lovers
A wild sand and pebble bay set in a deep canyon, famous for its bohemian atmosphere of freedom, campsites, and the passing Lycian Way trail.

Kabak Cove is a unique spot on the Fethiye coast, attracting fans of secluded travel, yoga, and wilderness. Tucked inside a deep canyon surrounded by pine forests, it is completely isolated from the noise of major resorts. There are no large chain hotels here; instead, accommodation is limited to wooden bungalows, yoga retreats, and campsites.

The beach is wide, composed of pebbles mixed with sand. The open sea features a deep turquoise color, but the depth increases very quickly and strong waves are common, so swim with caution. A mountain stream flows through the canyon, forming scenic waterfalls deeper in the valley, which can be reached via hiking paths.

Getting down to the cove is part of the adventure. By standard car, you can only reach the top of the canyon in the village of Faralya (where paid parking is available). The descent to the beach is a very steep dirt road, accessible only by local 4x4 shuttle trucks or via a steep hike on foot.

### Practical Information
- Location: Faralya Village, 30 km south of [Fethiye](/en/fethiye) (approx. 45 minutes by car)
- Road: Asphalt to the top of the canyon; steep dirt road down to the beach (accessible only on foot or via paid local 4x4 shuttle trucks)
- Infrastructure: Basic toilets and showers near the beach, sunbeds and umbrellas at private venues, small rustic cafes and bungalows in the pine grove
- Entrance: Free (parking at the top and the 4x4 shuttle transfer down are paid)
- Tip: If hiking down, wear sturdy athletic shoes and bring water. Do not attempt to drive down in a rental sedan, as you are highly likely to damage the chassis.
