# Blue Lagoon (Mavi Lagün) — Scenic Nature Reserve and the Trademark of Ölüdeniz
The famous sheltered bay featuring turquoise water and the sandy Kumburnu Spit, completely protected from waves and perfect for a peaceful swim.

The Blue Lagoon is the signature sight of both Ölüdeniz and Turkey's Turquoise Coast. It is a calm body of water separated from the open sea by the Kumburnu sandy spit. As a result, the lagoon is completely sheltered from big waves, and the water warms up quickly and stays warm longer. The area is protected as a national park (Ölüdeniz Tabiat Parkı), meaning there is an entrance fee.

The spit divides the beach area into two distinct sides. The inner shore faces the quiet, shallow waters of the lagoon, making it a perfect natural wading pool for families with toddlers. The outer shore faces the open sea, where the depth increases rapidly and waves roll in, similar to the adjacent [Belcekiz Beach](/en/fethiye/beach/belcekiz).

Inside the park, all amenities for a full-day visit are available. There are cafes and restaurants, along with showers, toilets, and changing cabins. Visitors can rent sunbeds and umbrellas, as well as pedal boats or canoes to explore the calm lagoon. The nearby Mount [Babadağ](/en/fethiye/todo/babadag) serves as the launch point for paragliders flying overhead.

### Practical Information
- Location: Ölüdeniz, 15 km south of [Fethiye](/en/fethiye)
- Road: Asphalt (accessible by car, taxi, or dolmuş to the final stop in Ölüdeniz)
- Infrastructure: Cafes, restaurants, toilets, showers, changing cabins, sunbed and umbrella rentals, pedal boat and canoe rentals
- Entrance: Paid (pedestrian entry is approx. 150 TL, car entry including parking is approx. 750 TL)
- Tip: Arrive early in the morning to secure a good spot on the spit and avoid the crowds. Bring a snorkeling mask, as you can spot small fish near the rocky edges of the lagoon.
