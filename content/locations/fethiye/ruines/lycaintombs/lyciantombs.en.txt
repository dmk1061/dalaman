Lycian Rock Tombs of Fethiye — Eternal Balconies Overlooking the Sea
Above the busy streets, the port, and the tiled roofs of modern Fethiye (ancient Telmessos), its ancient guardians — the Lycian rock tombs — tower silently. Carved into the sheer cliff thousands of years ago, they are not just a historical monument, but an integral part of the soul and landscape of the city, its main "hallmark".

Main Feature: The Tomb of Amyntas
Among the dozens of burials dotting the cliff, one stands out for its grandeur and excellent preservation. This is the Tomb of Amyntas, dating back to the 4th century BC. From a distance, it looks like the facade of an elegant Ionic temple with two columns, carved directly into the stone.

An inscription in Ancient Greek can be read on the pediment: "Amyntas, son of Hermagios". This made it possible to identify whom this majestic "house of eternity" belonged to, presumably one of the rulers of Telmessos.

Unlike many other tombs, you can get close to the Tomb of Amyntas. For a small fee, you can climb the stairs cut into the rock (about 100 steps) to inspect the intricate carving and look inside the empty burial chamber. But the main reward for the climb is, of course, the best panoramic view of Fethiye, its marina, and the entire gulf.

Why in the Cliffs?
The Lycians believed that after death their souls were carried to heaven by winged creatures, so they built tombs as high as possible to facilitate this journey. The location of the tomb also reflected the social status of the deceased — the higher, the nobler the family. These rock necropolises were eternal "balconies" for the nobility, from which they could look at their city forever.

Atmosphere and Tips
Best time to visit: Early morning, when it is not yet so hot, or closer to evening.

Evening lighting: Be sure to look at the tombs after sunset. They are very beautifully and delicately illuminated, creating a magical and slightly mysterious atmosphere in the center of the city.

How to get there: The tombs can be seen from anywhere in the city center. You can reach the entrance on foot by following the "Rock Tombs" signs, or by taxi.

Who will be interested?
The Fethiye tombs are an attraction for everyone. They are a must-visit for lovers of history and archaeology. They will captivate photographers with their views, especially at sunset. And they will definitely impress any tourist who wants to touch the ancient history of the city and see it from a bird's-eye view. This is an ideal way to combine an educational excursion with a little physical activity and get magnificent souvenir photos.
