# Amyntas Rock Tombs (Amintas Kaya Mezarları) — Lycian Cliffside Tombs in the Heart of Fethiye
Ancient Lycian tombs of the 4th century BC, carved into a sheer cliff in the center of Fethiye, offering a stunning panoramic view of the entire bay.

Towering above the bustling streets and tiled roofs of modern [Fethiye](/en/fethiye) (ancient Telmessos) are its primary historical guardians — the Lycian rock tombs. Carved into the cliffs thousands of years ago, they serve as the city's signature landmark. The grandest and most famous among them is the Tomb of Amyntas, dating back to the 4th century BC. From a distance, it resembles the facade of a delicate Ionic temple with two columns, carved directly out of the monolith stone. An ancient Greek inscription engraved on the wall reads: "Amyntas, son of Hermagios."

A stone stairway of about 200 steps leads up to the tomb. Climbing it allows visitors to examine the fine craftsmanship up close and peer into the empty burial chamber. The Lycians constructed their tombs on high ground, believing that winged creatures carried the souls of the deceased to heaven, and that a higher tomb reflected a higher social status. The platform at the tomb offers the best panoramic view of the city, the marina, and the islands, which is particularly breathtaking at sunset. In the evening, the monument is beautifully illuminated.

### Practical Information
- Location: [Fethiye](/en/fethiye) city center, Kesikkapı neighborhood
- Road: Asphalt (accessed via narrow and steep city streets, followed by a climb on foot up the stairs)
- Infrastructure: Ticket office, viewpoint, evening illumination. Full city infrastructure is available at the foot of the hill
- Entrance: Paid (approx. 3 EUR / 100-110 TL, Turkish Museum Card / Müze Kart is valid)
- Tip: Sturdy, comfortable shoes are essential for climbing the stone steps. The best time to visit is just before sunset for great photos and to avoid the midday heat.
