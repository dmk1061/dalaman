
Article: Tlos — A Fortress on a Cliff and the House of Pegasus
Ancient Tlos, located on the top of a high rocky hill with a panoramic view of the Xanthos valley, impresses with its scale and variety of structures. Unlike many other cities that were abandoned in antiquity, Tlos remained inhabited until the 19th century, during the Ottoman Empire. This led to a unique blend of Lycian, Roman, Byzantine, and Ottoman architecture.

Main Feature: The Acropolis-Fortress and the Tomb of Bellerophon
Acropolis and Ottoman Fortress: The most dominating part of Tlos is its acropolis, a natural rock fortress. At the top, you can see the ruins of an Ottoman fort built in the 19th century by a local feudal lord nicknamed "Bloody Ali Agha". This fort was erected directly on the foundations of the ancient Lycian citadel.

Tomb of Bellerophon: At the foot of the acropolis, carved into the rock, is the most famous tomb of Tlos. It has a temple-like facade and is decorated with a relief depicting the mythical hero Bellerophon riding his winged horse Pegasus. According to legend, Tlos was the home of this famous hero.

A Walk Around the City: What to See?
The territory of Tlos is huge, and you can spend several hours here exploring:

Necropolis: The slopes of the acropolis are dotted with numerous Lycian rock tombs of various shapes and sizes.

Stadium: At the foot of the hill are the ruins of a large stadium where athletic contests were once held. Tlos was known as a sports center of the Lycian League.

Roman Baths (Thermae): A well-preserved bath complex with high arched windows overlooking the valley. Nearby, you can see the remains of the palaestra (sports school).

Theater: The ruins of a large theater, which is currently under active restoration.

Byzantine Basilica: The remains of an early Christian church, demonstrating the importance of the city in the Byzantine era as well.

Pros and Cons
Pros:

A huge and very diverse archaeological complex with monuments from different eras.

Impressive hilltop location with stunning panoramic views.

An interesting combination of ancient ruins and a later Ottoman fortress.

Far fewer tourists than in coastal ancient cities.

Cons:

The territory is very large, requiring a lot of time and physical effort to explore.

It can be very hot in summer, and there is little shade.

Some parts of the complex are under excavation and may be partially closed.

Who will be interested in Tlos?
Tlos is a place for true explorers and history lovers. It will appeal to those who have already seen the main coastal ruins and want to delve deeper into the history of Lycia. Its scale and diversity of eras are far more impressive than many other ancient cities. This is an ideal destination for a day trip from Fethiye, Kaş, or Kalkan, which is convenient to combine with a visit to the nearby Saklıkent Gorge.
