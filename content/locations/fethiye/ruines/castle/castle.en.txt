Fethiye Castle — The Best Panoramic View of the Turquoise Coast
Above the Lycian tombs, at the very top of the hill that dominates Fethiye, are the ruins of a medieval castle. Although not much remains of the fortification itself, its strategic location makes it a must-visit place for anyone who wants to see the city and its picturesque bay from a bird's-eye view.

History: From the Knights to the Present Day
It is believed that the hill on which the castle stands was the acropolis of ancient Telmessos. However, the walls we see today are the remains of a fortress built or significantly rebuilt in the 15th century by the Knights Hospitaller from the island of Rhodes. The castle served as an important defensive point controlling the entrance to the Fethiye Gulf. Over time and after several strong earthquakes, it fell into decay, and today only fragments of walls and a few towers have survived.

Main Feature: Panoramic View
The main reason to go up to the castle today is not so much to inspect the ruins, but to enjoy the breathtaking panoramic view. A 360-degree view opens up from the top of the hill:

The city of Fethiye: The entire city is in full view, with its red tiled roofs, mosques, and busy streets.

Fethiye Gulf: The huge bay dotted with yachts and islands in all its glory.

Marina: A picturesque harbor with snow-white yachts.

Mountains: The majestic mountains surrounding the city.

This is the best place in Fethiye for panoramic photography, especially at sunset.

How to Get There and What to Keep in Mind
The path: A steep, winding road leads to the castle, starting in the old town, not far from the Lycian tombs. You need to go up on foot.

Shoes: Be sure to wear comfortable, non-slip shoes.

Time to visit: It is best to go up in the morning or evening to avoid the daytime heat.

Safety: Be careful. There are no fences or railings at the top, and the slopes are very steep.

Entrance: Admission to the ruins is free.

Pros and Cons
Pros:

The best panoramic view of Fethiye.

Free access.

An interesting walk through the old streets of the city on the way up.

An opportunity to touch the history of different eras.

Cons:

Very little remains of the castle itself, mostly fragments of walls.

A steep and tiring climb, especially in the heat.

Lack of safety measures at the top.

Who will be interested in Fethiye Castle?
Visiting the castle is for active travelers, photographers, and anyone who is not afraid of a short climb for the sake of stunning views. If you want to take the best photos of Fethiye, see the whole city and bay from one point, and feel like you are on top of the world, then climbing to the ruins of the old fortress will be a small but very memorable adventure for you.
