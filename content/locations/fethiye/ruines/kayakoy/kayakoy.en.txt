Kayaköy — A Ghost Village Where Time Has Stood Still
Kayaköy is a huge open-air museum, a monument to human tragedy and friendship. Walking along its deserted, stone-paved streets, among hundreds of houses without roofs and windows, produces an incredibly strong emotional impression. It is not just ruins, but a place where you can literally hear the echo of the past.

History of the Abandoned Village
Until 1923, a large Greek village called Levissi flourished here. Turks and Greeks lived here side by side in peace and harmony for centuries. However, after the Greco-Turkish War, an agreement was signed on the compulsory population exchange between Greece and Turkey.

The entire Greek population of Levissi was forced to leave their homes and move to Greece. In their place, Turks repatriated from Greece were supposed to settle. But they, being mostly farmers, could not adapt to the rocky soil and gradually left this place too, moving to more fertile valleys. Thus, the prosperous village turned into a ghost.

A Walk in Silence: What to See?
Stone houses: The main "sight" is the landscape itself, consisting of more than 500 (and according to some sources, up to 1000) abandoned stone houses cascading down the hillside.

Churches: Two large structures stand out among the ruins — these are two churches. The Lower Church (Kato Panagia) and the Upper Church (Taxiarchis). Despite the semi-ruined state, one can still see the remains of frescoes in them and feel the former grandeur.

Chapel on the top: A climb to the very top of the hill, to a small chapel, is rewarded with a stunning panoramic view of the entire valley and the sea in the distance.

Atmosphere and "New" Kayaköy
The atmosphere in the "old" town is very quiet, melancholic, and thought-provoking. This is a place for leisurely walks and reflections.

At the foot of the hill, in the valley, "new" Kayaköy exists today. This is a residential village with several excellent restaurants famous for their lamb cooked in an oven (tandır) and gözleme flatbreads. There are also many craft workshops and small boutique hotels. This contrast between the silence of the "dead" town on the hill and the life at its foot makes a strong impression.

Pros and Cons
Pros:

An incredibly powerful, atmospheric, and touching place.

A unique historical monument of the 20th century.

Very photogenic.

An opportunity to touch a complex and important part of the history of Turkey and Greece.

Cons:

The sad and melancholic atmosphere may not suit everyone.

Requires a lot of walking on uneven stone paths, often uphill.

It is very hot in summer, with practically no shade on the slopes.

Who will be interested in Kayaköy?
Kayaköy is a must-visit for thoughtful travelers interested in history, culture, and human destinies. It will appeal to photographers and everyone looking for something more than just a beach holiday. This is not an entertainment attraction, but a place for a deep emotional and historical experience that is remembered for a lifetime.
