Fethiye, located on the site of the ancient Lycian city of Telmessos, is one of the most famous tourist centers in Turkey. The city is situated on the Mediterranean coast, just 50 km from Dalaman International Airport, and is famous for its picturesque nature, rich history, and developed tourist infrastructure.

### History

The history of the city dates back to the 5th century BC. According to legend, the god Apollo fell in love with the daughter of the Phoenician king and, turning into a dog, won her heart. Their son was named Telmessos, which means "Land of Lights". The city was part of the Persian Empire, was a member of the Delian League, and in the 4th century BC was conquered by Alexander the Great.

During the Byzantine period, the city, known as Makri ("long"), flourished, but was abandoned due to the Arab-Byzantine wars. In the 15th century, it became part of the Ottoman Empire. The city received its modern name — Fethiye — in 1934 in honor of Fethi Bey, one of the first pilots of the Ottoman Air Force.

After a devastating earthquake in 1958, the city was almost completely rebuilt.

### Modern Fethiye

Today, Fethiye is a city with an international atmosphere, attracting tourists from all over the world. There are three modern marinas here, hosting both private yachts and cruise liners. In the city and its surroundings, you can find everything for a complete vacation:

*   **Historical Sights:** Lycian rock tombs, including the Tomb of Amyntas, and ruins of a Byzantine fortress.
*   **Natural Beauties:** The unique beach of Ölüdeniz, the picturesque Butterfly Valley, and the Saklıkent Gorge.
*   **Active Recreation:** Paragliding from Mount Babadağ, diving, jeep and quad safari, hiking along the famous Lycian Way.
*   **Entertainment:** Numerous restaurants, bars, clubs, and traditional markets.

Fethiye is a unique place where you can find ancient sarcophagi on the city streets, and modern life bustles against the backdrop of ancient temple ruins.
