Fethiye and its surroundings are rich in both historical monuments and unique natural sites. Here you can touch the heritage of ancient civilizations and enjoy incredible landscapes.

### Historical Sights

*   [🏛️ <u>**Lycian Rock Tombs**</u>](/fethiye/sight/lycaintombs): Carved into the cliff above the city, these tombs are the symbol of Fethiye. The most famous and majestic of them is the **Tomb of Amyntas** (4th century BC), which resembles the facade of an Ionic temple. A magnificent panoramic view of the city and the bay opens up from the hill where the tombs are located.

*   [👻 <u>**Ghost Village of Kayaköy**</u>](/fethiye/sight/kayakoy): Located 8 km from Fethiye is the abandoned Greek village of Levissi, now known as Kayaköy. It is a real open-air museum with hundreds of stone houses, churches, and chapels. Walking along the narrow streets of this "ghost town" leaves an unforgettable impression.

*   [🏺 <u>**Ancient City of Tlos**</u>](/fethiye/sight/tlos): One of the oldest and most important cities of the Lycian League, located about 40 km from Fethiye. Here you can see an acropolis with an Ottoman fortress, a stadium, Roman baths, and, of course, impressive rock tombs.

*   **Xanthos and Letoon:** Xanthos was the capital of ancient Lycia, and Letoon was its main religious center. Both sites are included in the UNESCO World Heritage List. Ruins of temples, a theater, agoras, and unique Lycian tombstones are preserved here.

*   [🏰 <u>**Fethiye Castle**</u>](/fethiye/sight/castle): The medieval fortress overlooking the city was built by the Knights of the Order of Saint John. Beautiful views of the harbor and the city open up from here.

### Natural Sights

*   [🏔️ <u>**Saklıkent Gorge**</u>](/fethiye/sight/saklikent): One of the deepest gorges in the world, located 50 km from Fethiye. Its length is 18 km, and the wall height reaches 300 meters. In summer, when the water level drops, you can take a refreshing walk along the bottom of the gorge.

*   [🦋 <u>**Butterfly Valley (Kelebekler Vadisi)**</u>](/fethiye/beach/kelebekler): A picturesque valley and beach, accessible mainly from the sea. It got its name due to the colonies of tiger butterflies. This is an ideal place for those seeking privacy and the beauty of wild nature.

*   [🪂 <u>**Mount Babadağ**</u>](/fethiye/sight/babadag): A majestic mountain towering over Ölüdeniz. This is one of the best places in the world for paragliding. Even if you do not decide to fly, it is worth going up to the top by cable car to enjoy the breathtaking views of the Blue Lagoon.
