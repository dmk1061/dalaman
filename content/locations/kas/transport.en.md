Kaş is a compact town, but its remote location and many interesting places in the surroundings make transport an important issue for trip planning.

### How to Get to Kaş

Kaş is located at a significant distance from major airports, which helps it preserve its unique, slow-paced atmosphere.

*   **By Plane:**
    *   **Dalaman Airport (DLM):** The nearest airport to Kaş, located about 150 km to the west. The journey by car or transfer takes about 2 hours.
    *   **Antalya Airport (AYT):** Located about 200 km east of Kaş. Travel time is about 3 hours.

    From both airports, Kaş can be reached by shuttles (Havaş or private transfer companies, which should be booked in advance), rental car, or intercity buses with transfers at Fethiye or Antalya bus stations.

*   **By Bus:** Kaş has its own bus station (otogar), where buses arrive from major cities such as Antalya, Fethiye, Izmir, and Istanbul. The company `Batı Antalya Tur` operates regular services along the coast between Fethiye and Antalya, stopping in Kaş.

### Getting Around the Town and Surroundings

*   **On Foot:** The center of Kaş is very compact and is best explored on foot. All major shops, restaurants, and town attractions are within walking distance.

*   **Dolmuş:** Local minibuses are the main way to get around the surrounding area. From the bus station, dolmuşes regularly depart to:
    *   The Çukurbağ Peninsula (Çukurbağ Yarımadası), where many hotels and beach clubs are located.
    *   Nearby beaches, including the famous Kaputaş Beach.
    *   The neighboring town of Kalkan.

*   **Taxi:** There are several taxi stands in the town. This is a convenient but more expensive way to get around, especially for trips to remote locations or at night.

*   **Ferry to Meis Island (Kastellorizo):** Ferries depart daily (during the tourist season) from Kaş harbor to the Greek island of Meis. The trip takes only 20-30 minutes and is a great option for a day trip. A Schengen visa is required for this trip.

*   **Vehicle Rental:** In Kaş, you can rent a car, scooter, or bicycle. This is the best option for those who want to independently explore the numerous ancient cities, secluded coves, and picturesque mountain villages of the Lycian coast.
