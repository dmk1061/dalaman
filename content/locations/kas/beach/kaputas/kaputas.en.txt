Kaputaş Beach – A Turquoise Pearl at the Mouth of the Canyon
Kaputaş Beach is not just a beach; it is a natural wonder and an icon of the Turkish coast. Its photographs adorn countless guidebooks and advertising brochures, and for good reason. Located at the mouth of a deep canyon that cuts its way to the sea, Kaputaş captivates the imagination with its unreal, dazzlingly turquoise water.

Key Feature: Water Color and Location
Unreal turquoise: The secret of Kaputaş's incredible water color lies in the cold underground springs that emerge from the canyon and mix with sea water. This mixture of fresh and salty water, along with the light sandy-pebbly bottom, creates the famous shade that looks as if it were edited in a photo editor.

Beach in a canyon: Kaputaş is squeezed between two high, sheer cliffs, creating a sense of privacy and grandeur. The view of the beach from above, from the D400 highway, is one of the most famous panoramas in Turkey.

Beach and Atmosphere
Shoreline: The beach consists of a mixture of golden sand and fine white pebbles.

Sea: The water here is cool because of the springs. The sea is open, so there are often waves, which adds fun to swimming but requires caution. The depth increases rather quickly.

Atmosphere: In season, it is very lively and crowded. This beach is a real "star," and everyone wants to visit it. To enjoy it in relative peace, you should arrive early in the morning.

How to Get There: 187 Steps to Paradise
The beach is located directly below the busy D400 highway connecting Kaş and Kalkan.

Descent: To get to the beach, you have to overcome a long staircase of nearly 200 steps. The descent is easy and pleasant, as more and more beautiful views open up at every step. However, the climb back up in the midday heat can be a real test.

Transport: You can get there by minibuses (dolmuşes) that regularly run between Kaş and Kalkan, or by private car. Parking is very limited and is just an extension of the roadside along the highway.

Pros and Cons
Pros:

One of the most beautiful beaches in the world with an incredible water color.

A stunningly photogenic place.

Availability of basic infrastructure (municipal cafe, toilets, showers).

Cons:

A long staircase, which can be a problem for people with disabilities and families with small children.

Very crowded in high season.

Frequent waves and fast depth require attention when swimming.

Very limited parking.

Who is Kaputaş suitable for?
Kaputaş is a "must-see" destination. It is a mandatory visit for anyone traveling along the Lycian coast. It will especially appeal to photographers, romantics, and anyone ready to make a little effort to swim in water of an unreal turquoise color. Despite the difficulties with parking and the stairs, the beauty of this place completely compensates for all inconveniences and leaves memories for a lifetime.
