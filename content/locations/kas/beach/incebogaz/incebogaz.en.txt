# İnceboğaz Beach (İnceboğaz Plajı) — Swimming in Two Different Seas of Kaş

İnceboğaz Beach is a unique double-sided shore on a narrow isthmus in Kaş, offering visitors a choice between a calm harbor and the open, wavy sea.

Located at the narrowest point connecting the Çukurbağ Peninsula to the mainland, this geographic spot lets you choose your swimming conditions by simply crossing the road.

### The Two Sides of the Isthmus
The name İnceboğaz means "thin throat" in Turkish. The road runs along the ridge of the isthmus, separating two completely different beach areas:
1. **The Inner Beach (Bucak Bay):** Facing the sheltered bay and Kaş Marina, the water here is exceptionally calm, lake-like, and slightly warmer. It is a highly popular and safe spot for families with young children.
2. **The Outer Beach:** Facing the open Mediterranean Sea and the Greek island of Kastellorizo (Meis). The water here is cooler and more refreshing, with occasional waves on windy days.

Both sides feature small pebbles and a relatively gradual entry into the water. The beach holds a Blue Flag certification for its water quality. The inner beach is managed by the local municipality and equipped with basic services, while the outer side is more natural and suited for those who prefer to relax with their own towels.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** [Kaş](/en/kas) region, at the start of the Çukurbağ Peninsula (Çukurbağ Yarımadası), about 1.5 km from the town center
- **Road:** Asphalt (easily reachable on foot in 15–20 minutes from the center, or via taxi and local minibuses heading to the peninsula)
- **Infrastructure:** Municipal cafe with budget-friendly food and drinks, changing cabins, showers, toilets, sunbed and umbrella rentals
- **Entrance:** Free (sunbed and umbrella rentals are paid; free parking is available along the roadside)
- **Tip:** Bring water shoes for a more comfortable walk on the pebbles. If you find one side too windy, simply walk across the road to the other side where it is likely to be calm.
