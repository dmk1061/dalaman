# Küçük Çakıl Beach (Küçük Çakıl Plajı) — An Icy Oasis in the Center of Kaş

Küçük Çakıl Beach is a tiny pebble cove in central Kaş, famous for its icy freshwater springs and trendy beachfront platforms that surround the bay.

Located just a short walk from the main town harbor, this small inlet is not meant for long sunbathing sessions, but rather for a quick, incredibly refreshing plunge into crystal-clear waters.

### Beach Features and the Surrounding Beach Clubs
The defining characteristic of Küçük Çakıl is the presence of cold freshwater springs bubbling up from the seabed and running down the cliffs. This keeps the water temperature bracingly cool, even during the hottest summer months. Due to the pebbly bottom and the spring water, the sea here boasts an intense emerald-turquoise hue and remarkable transparency.

The public pebble strip is very small and has no facilities on its own. Consequently, most visitors prefer to relax at the private beach clubs (such as Derya Beach Club and Çınarlar Beach Club) flanking the cove. These clubs feature wooden platforms built over the rocks, offering sunbeds, umbrellas, ladders into the water, and full restaurant service.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** Central [Kaş](/en/kas), just east of the main harbor
- **Road:** Asphalt (easily accessible on foot from anywhere in town, a 5–10 minute walk from Uzun Çarşı)
- **Infrastructure:** Beach clubs with sunbeds, umbrellas, dining, showers, and toilets (no facilities are present on the public pebble strip)
- **Entrance:** Free for the public pebble area (the adjacent beach clubs are free to enter but operate on a minimum spend deposit for food and drinks)
- **Tip:** The cove is not recommended for toddlers due to the cold water, rocky entry, and deep sea. Wearing water shoes is advised, and arriving early is key to securing a spot at the beach clubs in summer.
