# Antiphellos Ancient Theatre (Antiphellos Antik Tiyatrosu) — A Sunset Stage in Kaş

Antiphellos Ancient Theatre in Kaş is a well-preserved 1st-century BC structure renowned for its spectacular open-air sunset views over the sea.

As the most significant surviving monument of the ancient Lycian port city of Antiphellos, this theater is integrated directly into the western edge of modern Kaş.

### Architectural Features and the Sunset Tradition
Built in the 1st century BC and later restored during the Roman period (2nd century AD), the theater was designed to accommodate around 4,000 spectators. It features 26 rows of stone seats carved directly into the hillside. The unique feature of this theater is the absence of a permanent stone stage building (skene), leaving the view of the Mediterranean Sea and the Greek island of Kastellorizo (Meis) completely unobstructed.

Today, the theater is the center of a beloved local tradition. Every evening, travelers and locals walk up to the top rows of the theater to watch the sunset over the sea. The atmosphere during sunset is remarkably calm and peaceful. During the day, it serves as an excellent panoramic lookout point, and it occasionally hosts outdoor concerts and cultural festivals.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** Western edge of [Kaş](/en/kas), along the road to the Çukurbağ Peninsula
- **Road:** Asphalt (an easy 5–10 minute walk from the main town square or harbor along the coastal road)
- **Infrastructure:** Benches and trash bins are available at the site. Standard city facilities (restaurants, restrooms) are within walking distance.
- **Entrance:** Free (accessible 24/7)
- **Tip:** Arrive about 30–45 minutes before sunset to secure a good seat on the upper stone tiers. Since there is no shade, bring a hat and water if you are visiting during the day.
