Ancient Theater of Kas — A Stage for Sunset Over the Sea
Just a 5-10 minute walk from the central square of Kas, on a hillside facing the sea, lies a beautifully preserved Hellenistic theater. This is the most significant structure remaining from the ancient Lycian port city of Antiphellos, on the site of which modern Kas stands.

Main Feature: Sea View and Sunset Atmosphere
Unlike many other ancient theaters, the theater in Kas is unique in its location. It is built so that its stage and audience rows face directly toward the Mediterranean Sea and the Greek island of Meis (Kastellorizo). It is not just ruins, but one of the best viewing platforms in the city.

The main "tradition" associated with this place is to watch the sunset here. Every evening, dozens of people — both tourists and locals — gather on the upper rows of the theater to silently watch the sun slowly sink into the sea, painting the sky and water in incredible colors. The atmosphere in these moments is very calm, romantic, and slightly magical.

History and Architecture
Age: The theater was built in the 1st century BC and later, in the Roman period, it was reconstructed.

Capacity: It was designed to hold approximately 4,000 spectators.

Condition: The theater is perfectly preserved. The 26 rows of spectator seats have been carefully restored, and today concerts and cultural events are sometimes held here.

Access: Entrance to the theater is completely free, and it is open for visits at any time.

Pros and Cons
Pros:

Stunning panoramic view of the sea and the island of Meis.
An ideal spot for watching the sunset.
Excellent preservation and the opportunity to sit on ancient stones.
Free and open access at any time.
Located within walking distance of the city center.

Cons:

There is practically no shade during the day, so it can be uncomfortable in the heat.
It is not as large-scale a complex as the theaters in Xanthos or Myra, but its charm lies precisely in its intimacy and the view.

Who Will Find the Theater Interesting?
The Ancient Theater of Kas is an attraction for everyone. It is a must-visit for romantic souls, photographers, and anyone who wants to find the best place to watch the sunset. It is also a great place for those interested in history who do not want to go on a long excursion. Simply come here an hour before sunset, bring a bottle of water, climb to the top row, and become a spectator of the most beautiful performance that nature itself stages every evening.
