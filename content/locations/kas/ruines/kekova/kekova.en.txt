The Sunken City of Kekova – The Atlantis of the Lycian Coast
Kekova is not just an archaeological site; it is one of the most poetic and mysterious places in Turkey. Imagine the crystal-clear turquoise waters of the Mediterranean Sea, through which the outlines of ancient walls, paved streets, stairs descending into the depths, and fragments of amphorae are clearly visible. This is a place where natural beauty and tragic history have merged into a single, mesmerizing landscape.

Key Feature: Antiquity Under Water
History of the submersion: In the 2nd century AD, a series of powerful earthquakes occurred in this region. The ground literally gave way beneath the feet of the inhabitants of the ancient city of Dolichiste, located on Kekova Island. The city partially sank under water, and the surviving residents abandoned their homes.

Today, Kekova is a protected archaeological zone. Swimming and diving directly next to the ruins are prohibited here to preserve the historical monument. However, excursion boats come very close to the shore, moving at minimum speed, which allows visitors to view the sunken ruins. Many boats are equipped with a glass bottom for a better view.

What Will You See During the Trip?
The Sunken City: Half-ruined walls of houses, the remains of a Byzantine church, stone stairs descending directly into the water, and the ancient harbor.

Simena Fortress (Kaleköy): Located opposite the island, on the mainland. This is a picturesque village with no road access. At the top of the hill rises a medieval fortress built on Lycian foundations. It offers one of the most beautiful views of the entire Kekova Bay. In the village itself, you can try unique local homemade ice cream (for example, cactus or fig flavor).

Lycian Sarcophagi: The hallmark of Kekova is the single sarcophagi standing directly in the water near the shore. They have become one of the most recognizable symbols of Turkey's southern coast.

How to Get There?
Most tourists visit Kekova as part of a day trip:

From Kaş by boat: Large excursion boats depart daily from the port of Kaş. The trip takes the whole day and includes swimming in picturesque coves, lunch on the boat, and a visit to Simena.

From Kaş by car + boat: You can drive to the village of Üçağız, which is closer to Kekova (about 40 minutes), and hire a boat there for a short tour.

Pros and Cons
Pros:

A unique historical site that has no analogues in the region.

Incredible natural beauty, pure water, and scenic coves for swimming.

A visit to the colorful village of Simena with its fortress and homemade ice cream.

A great option for a full-day sea voyage.

Cons:

Swimming directly next to the sunken ruins is prohibited.

At the peak of the season, there can be many tourist boats in the bay.

A full-day excursion can be tiring for small children.

Who will like Kekova?
Kekova is a must-visit for anyone who loves history, archaeology, and beautiful boat trips. It is the perfect combination of an educational holiday and a relaxed day on the water. If you want to see the legends of Atlantis come to life and enjoy stunning views of the Lycian coast, a trip to Kekova will be one of the highlights of your holiday in Kaş.
