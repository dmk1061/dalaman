# Patara Ruins (Patara Antik Kenti) — The Birthplace of Democracy and St. Nicholas

The Patara Ruins are the ancient capital of the Lycian League, the birthplace of St. Nicholas, and a grand archaeological park surrounded by sand dunes.

As a major harbor and political hub of ancient Lycia, Patara's extensive ruins constitute one of the most significant and visually stunning archaeological parks in Turkey.

### Historical Significance and Key Sites
The crown jewel of Patara is the Bouleuterion (parliament building of the Lycian League). Here, representatives from Lycian cities gathered to govern the world's first democratic federal union. The building has been meticulously restored to its ancient grandeur. Another notable monument is the lighthouse built by Emperor Nero in the 1st century AD — one of the oldest surviving lighthouses in the world, reconstructed using its original stone blocks.

Patara also holds immense religious importance as the birthplace of Saint Nicholas (born around 270 AD), who later became the bishop of neighboring Myra. Other key sights include the triumphal Arch of Mettius Modestus, a large ancient theater partially buried under drifting sand, Roman baths, and the colonnaded streets leading to the ancient harbor, which has now transitioned into a marshy lake.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** [Kaş](/en/kas) region, about 43 km west of Kaş and 15 km from Kalkan, near Gelemiş village
- **Road:** Asphalt (accessible via the D400 highway and Gelemiş village road). A large free parking lot is available at the entrance of the archaeological site.
- **Infrastructure:** Ticket booth, information signs, souvenir shop, restrooms near the entrance. Shade is scarce within the ruins, but a municipal cafe is available near the beach.
- **Entrance:** Paid. The combined admission ticket for the ruins and the beach costs €15 for foreign visitors (free for Museum Pass Türkiye holders).
- **Tip:** Visit the ruins in the late afternoon (after 4:00 PM) to avoid the intense midday heat. After exploring, head to the nearby dunes to watch a scenic sunset over [Patara Beach](/en/kas/beach/patara). Wear comfortable walking shoes, and bring water and a hat.
