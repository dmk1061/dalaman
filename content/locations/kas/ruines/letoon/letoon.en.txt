The Sacred Heart of Lycia and the Home of Goddess Leto
In a fertile valley just a few kilometers from the ancient capital of Xanthos lies Letoon – the federal sanctuary of the Lycian League. It was not a place to live, but a place of worship. People gathered here from all over Lycia to honor the goddess Leto and her divine twin children – Apollo and Artemis.

The Legend of the Goddess Leto
According to Greek mythology, the Titaness Leto, having become pregnant by Zeus, was forced to wander the world, escaping the wrath of the jealous Hera. It was in these lands, in Lycia, that she found refuge to give birth to her children. According to one version of the myth, she wanted to bathe her newborns in a local spring, but shepherds chased her away. In anger, Leto turned them into frogs. This sacred spring became the center of worship, around which the Letoon sanctuary grew. Even today, walking through the ruins, you will constantly hear the croaking of frogs, which gives this place a special atmosphere.

Walking Through the Sanctuary: What to See?
Three Temples: The main attraction of Letoon is the foundations of three temples standing side-by-side. The largest, western temple was dedicated to Leto herself. The smaller central one was dedicated to her daughter, Artemis. And the eastern one, with a beautifully preserved mosaic of a lyre on the floor, was dedicated to her son, Apollo.

Nymphaeum and Sacred Spring: The most picturesque part of the ruins. This is a rectangular basin fed by a natural spring, which over time rose and flooded part of the ancient buildings. The sight of semi-submerged columns and walls reflecting in the water is incredibly beautiful.

Theater: On the hillside is a well-preserved Hellenistic theater that was used for religious ceremonies and festivities.

Trilingual Stele: It was here that one of the most important linguistic discoveries in history was made – a stele with an inscription in three languages (Lycian, Ancient Greek, and Aramaic). It became the key to deciphering the dead Lycian language (now kept in the Fethiye Museum).

Pros and Cons
Pros:

Huge historical and mythological significance.

Very atmospheric and peaceful place with no crowds of tourists.

Picturesque ruins, partially flooded with water.

UNESCO World Heritage status, highlighting its importance.

Cons:

The area can be swampy, especially in spring.

To fully appreciate the significance of the ruins, it is advisable to have some understanding of mythology and history.

It is a small archaeological park that can be explored in an hour or an hour and a half.

Who will be interested in Letoon?
Letoon is a place for connoisseurs of history, mythology, and quiet, meditative walks. It is almost always visited in conjunction with neighboring Xanthos to get a complete picture of the life and beliefs of ancient Lycia. If you want to take a break from noisy beaches and touch ancient secrets and the spiritual origins of an entire civilization, then a trip to Letoon will leave you with very deep and meaningful impressions.
