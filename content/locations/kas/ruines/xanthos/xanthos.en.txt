# Xanthos Ruins (Xanthos Antik Kenti) — The Tragic Capital of Ancient Lycia

Xanthos Ruins are the ancient capital of Lycia and a UNESCO World Heritage site, famous for its magnificent tombs and tragic history of self-sacrifice.

Perched on a hill overlooking the Eşen River valley, this once-mighty city entered history through two acts of mass suicide, where its citizens chose destruction over surrender to Persian and Roman invaders.

### Archaeological Treasures and Monumental Tombs
Xanthos is a treasure trove of Lycian funerary art, home to unique pillar tombs and sarcophagi resting on high pedestals. While the two most famous monuments — the Harpy Tomb (featuring relief carvings of mythical creatures) and the Nereid Monument (styled like a Greek temple) — were removed in the 19th century and are now housed in the British Museum, their grand original foundations, replicas, and numerous other structures remain on-site.

As you explore, you will also see a well-preserved Roman theater, the ruins of the Lycian and Roman acropolises, an ancient marketplace (agora), and a Byzantine basilica with mosaic floors. Another vital monument is the Xanthian Obelisk (Xanthos Stele), a stone pillar bearing the longest surviving inscription in the Lycian language, which was crucial to its decipherment.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** Border of Muğla and Antalya provinces, about 46 km west of [Kaş](/en/kas) and 65 km south of Fethiye, near Kınık village
- **Road:** Asphalt (easily reached by driving off the D400 highway at Kınık). A small free parking lot is located at the entrance.
- **Infrastructure:** Ticket booth, restrooms, information panels. There is no shade or drinking water inside the ruins.
- **Entrance:** Paid. The ticket costs approximately 200 TL (free with Museum Pass Türkiye).
- **Tip:** Combine your trip with a visit to [Letoon Sanctuary](/en/kas/ruines/letoon), located just 4 km away. Wear sturdy sports shoes to navigate the uneven and rocky paths, and bring plenty of water, sunglasses, and sunblock.
