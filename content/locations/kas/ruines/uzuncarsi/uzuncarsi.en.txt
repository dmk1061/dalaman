King's Tomb — The Silent Guardian on Kas's Main Street
At the very end of Kas's main shopping street, Uzun Carsi, where souvenir shops give way to cozy restaurants, stands a monumental and unexpected monument — a freestanding Lycian sarcophagus from the 4th century BC. Known as the "King's Tomb" or the "Lion Sarcophagus," it is not just a landmark, but the true heart and symbol of the city.

Main Feature: Fusion of Eras
The most striking thing about this tomb is its location. It does not stand in the silence of a museum or on the grounds of an archaeological park. It is an active participant in city life. Thousands of tourists pass by it every day, children play nearby, and coffee is sipped in neighboring cafes. This sarcophagus is clear proof of how in Kas, the past is not separated from the present, but is an integral part of it.

Architecture and Mysteries
Monumentality: The tomb is carved from a single block of stone and consists of two parts: a base and a massive lid in a characteristic Lycian style resembling an overturned boat.

Lion Heads: It received its name "Lion Sarcophagus" because of the four relief carvings of lion heads on the lid, which were probably intended to guard the peace of the deceased.

Mysterious Inscription: On the base of the sarcophagus, an inscription in the Lycian language has been preserved, which has not yet been fully deciphered. Therefore, we still do not know exactly who was buried in this majestic monument, which only adds to its mystery. Despite the name "King's," there is no exact proof that a ruler rested here.

What to Do at the Tomb?
Stop and Admire: Simply stop in the middle of a busy street and realize that you are standing next to a monument that is almost 2,500 years old.

Examine the Details: Get closer to look at the intricate carvings, lion heads, and mysterious writings.

Take a Photo: This is a must-photograph spot in Kas. The sarcophagus looks especially impressive in the evening when the backlight is turned on against the backdrop of the lively lights of the restaurants.

Summary: The "Tomb in Uzun Carsi" is not just a stone from the past. It is the soul of Kas, its signature landmark, and a constant reminder of the rich and mysterious history of the Lycian land that continues to live in the very heart of this modern and vibrant city.
