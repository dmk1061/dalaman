# Uzun Çarşı Street and the King's Sarcophagus in Kaş

Uzun Çarşı is a picturesque pedestrian street in Kaş, lined with historic Greek houses, boutiques, and topped by the ancient Lycian Lion Sarcophagus.

It is the most charming and Instagram-famous street in town, where historic architecture, vibrant bougainvillea, and lively resort culture blend seamlessly.

### The Atmosphere of the Street and the Lion Sarcophagus
Strolling up Uzun Çarşı (which translates to "Long Bazaar"), you will pass beautifully restored wooden balconies of traditional Greek-style houses. Today, these buildings house local craft shops, jewelry boutiques, spice stalls, and cozy cafes. The cobblestone street climbs steadily uphill, leading directly to its crowning jewel: the King's Sarcophagus (also known as the Lion Sarcophagus or Aslanlı Lahit).

Dating back to the 4th century BC, this monument is carved from a single block of stone. It gets its name from the four sculpted lion heads adorning its gothic-style lid, which resembles an upturned boat hull — a classic design of Lycian burials. The lower tier of the sarcophagus features an undeciphered inscription in the Lycian language, meaning the identity of the noble person buried here remains a mystery.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** [Kaş](/en/kas) historic center, Uzun Çarşı Street
- **Road:** Pedestrian-only cobblestone street. The street slopes gently upwards from the main town square and harbor area.
- **Infrastructure:** Numerous restaurants, cafes, clothing boutiques, souvenir shops, and boutique hotels line the street.
- **Entrance:** Free (the street and the monument are accessible 24/7)
- **Tip:** Visit between 8:00 AM and 9:00 AM to photograph the street and the sarcophagus without tourist crowds. In the evening, the street comes alive with warm lighting and outdoor dining. Wear flat, comfortable shoes for walking on the cobblestones.
