# Lycian Rock Tombs of Kaş (Kaş Kaya Mezarları) — Ancient Monuments in the Town Center

The Lycian Rock Tombs of Kaş are ancient cliffside vaults and a famous Lion Sarcophagus integrated directly into the town streets, free to visit anytime.

Unlike many archaeological parks, these remnants of ancient Antiphellos are not fenced off, standing harmoniously alongside modern boutiques and cafes.

### Hillside Tombs and the King's Sarcophagus
While strolling through the town, you will encounter two distinct types of Lycian graves:
1. **Rock-Cut Tombs:** Carved into the steep cliffside rising directly above the residential areas. Imposing by day, they are beautifully illuminated at night, casting a mystical glow visible from almost anywhere in the center.
2. **The King's Sarcophagus (Lion Tomb / Aslanlı Lahit):** Located at the top of the popular [Uzun Çarşı](/en/kas/ruines/uzuncarsi) shopping street. Dating back to the 4th century BC, this monument features four carved lion heads on its massive gothic-style lid.

These monuments require no tickets and are fully accessible, creating a unique urban landscape where daily life seamlessly blends with three millennia of history.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** [Kaş](/en/kas) center, Andifli hillside (rock tombs) and Uzun Çarşı Street (Lion Sarcophagus)
- **Road:** Asphalt and cobblestone pedestrian streets. The climb to the rock tombs involves ascending urban streets and stone stairs.
- **Infrastructure:** Full town amenities (restaurants, shops, public toilets nearby)
- **Entrance:** Free (accessible 24/7)
- **Tip:** Wear comfortable shoes with good grip if you plan to walk up the uneven, rocky trails closer to the hillside tombs. To take photos of the Lion Sarcophagus without crowds, visit early in the morning.
