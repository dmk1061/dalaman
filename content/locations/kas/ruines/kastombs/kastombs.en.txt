Rock Tombs of Kaş – Ancient Neighbors of a Modern Town
A walk through Kaş is a constant feeling of a dialogue between times. Modern boutiques, cozy cafes, and lively bars here live side-by-side with silent witnesses of a thousand-year history – Lycian tombs. They are not set apart in a separate museum complex, but are organically woven into the fabric of the town, making it absolutely unique.

Key Feature: History at Every Step
In Kaş (ancient Antiphellos), you can find two main types of Lycian burials:

1. Rock-cut tombs:
Right above the town center rises a rocky slope dotted with dozens of tombs. They look down upon the town and the sea, recalling the Lycian belief that the souls of the dead should be closer to the sky. By day, they impress with their scale, and in the evening, when the lighting turns on, they transform into a magical and somewhat mystical sight visible from any point in the center.

2. "The King's Tomb" (Aslanlı Lahit - Lion Sarcophagus):
This is, without a doubt, the most famous and photographed symbol of Kaş. Right at the top of the main shopping street, Uzun Çarşı, stands a majestic, free-standing sarcophagus from the 4th century BC. It is called the "Lion" tomb because of the four lion heads carved on the lid. It is striking in its location: you walk past souvenir shops and cafes, and suddenly this monumental monument of antiquity rises in front of you. This is a visual embodiment of the spirit of Kaş, where the past and present are inseparable.

How to See Them?
Rock tombs on the slope: They are impossible to miss. Just lift your head while walking through the town center. You can get closer to some of them by walking up the streets leading uphill from the main square.

The King's Tomb: Just walk to the end of the main shopping street, Uzun Çarşı.

Pros and Cons
Pros:

An incredible sense of merging eras, where antiquity is a part of everyday life.

Very photogenic, especially the illuminated tombs in the evening and the sarcophagus against the background of a lively street.

Absolutely free and accessible at any time of day.

Cons:

Unlike Fethiye, you cannot go inside the tombs.

These are more like elements of the urban landscape than a full-fledged archaeological park for long study.

Who will be interested in this?
The rock tombs of Kaş are not just a sight; they are the very essence of the town. Absolutely everyone who comes to Kaş will like them. This does not require a separate excursion or trip – you will constantly stumble upon these traces of antiquity during your walks. It is this amazing coexistence of a modern resort and an ancient necropolis that creates the unique, magical atmosphere for which Kaş is so loved.
