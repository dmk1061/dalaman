# Lake Beach (Köyceğiz Gölü Plajı) — Mountain-Framed Freshwater Oasis

Köyceğiz Lake Beach is a scenic recreation area with warm fresh water, surrounded by mountains and eucalyptus trees, offering calm swimming without any waves.

Lake [Köyceğiz](/en/koycegiz) is a massive tectonic body of water that serves as a wonderful alternative to the classic seaside. The local Lake Beach (Göl Plajı) attracts those looking to cool off in soft, fresh water, away from the salt and bustle of major coastal resorts.

### Water and Nature Features
The lake water is slightly mineralized, as the basin is fed by the famous [Sultaniye Hot Springs](/en/koycegiz/todo/sultanye), which are rich in radon and sulfur. Bathing here is believed to have beneficial effects on the skin and joints. Due to the lack of sea currents and tides, the water is always calm and warms up much faster than the sea. The beach is lined by a picturesque promenade with sprawling palms, majestic eucalyptus trees, and rare sweetgum (liquidambar) trees.

### Infrastructure
The shoreline is equipped with changing cabins, showers, and toilets. Visitors can rent sunbeds and umbrellas. Cozy tea gardens (Çay Bahçesi) and small cafes operate nearby, offering very affordable Turkish tea and snacks accompanied by lovely lake views.

### Things to Do
* **Water Sports:** Rent paddleboards (SUP), kayaks, and pedal boats right on the beach.
* **Walking:** Stroll along the shaded [Köyceğiz Seafront](/en/koycegiz/todo/seafront).
* **Sunset Watching:** Sunsets over Lake Köyceğiz are considered among the most beautiful on the coast — the sun slowly dips behind the mountain silhouettes, painting the water in gold and crimson shades.

### Practical Information
* **Location:** Along the town waterfront, within easy walking distance from Köyceğiz town center.
* **Road:** Flat, paved town street, easily accessible on foot, by bicycle, or by car.
* **Infrastructure:** Changing cabins, showers, toilets, sunbed rentals, watercraft rentals, cafes, and tea gardens.
* **Entrance:** Free. Town parking along the seafront is free or costs a nominal fee.
* **Tip:** During summer evenings, midges and mosquitoes tend to gather near the water, so bring a quality insect repellent for sunset walks.
