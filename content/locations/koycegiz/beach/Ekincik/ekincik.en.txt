Ekincik — Emerald bay for yachts and seekers of tranquility
Ekincik Bay, located approximately 35 km from Köyceğiz, is one of the most picturesque and peaceful spots on the coast. Surrounded by steep mountains thickly covered with pine forests, this bay is a favorite anchorage for yachtsmen sailing the legendary "Blue Cruise" (Mavi Yolculuk) route. Here, the Aegean Sea meets untouched nature, creating ideal conditions for a relaxing holiday far from civilization.

Key Features: Pine forests and yacht anchorage
Yacht Harbor: Ekincik is a famous port of call for luxury yachts and traditional Turkish gulet boats. The local marina is well protected from winds, making it an ideal place to anchor.

Virgin Nature: The bay is surrounded by pristine forest. The air here is filled with the aromas of pine needles, resin, and the sea, while the silence is broken only by the chirping of cicadas and the quiet splash of waves.

Gateway to Dalyan: It is from here that many yachtsmen transfer to small river boats (dolmuşes) to go on a tour along the Dalyan River, see the Lycian tombs and ancient Kaunos.

Beach and Infrastructure
Coastline: Ekincik beach is long, covered with a mixture of dark sand and pebbles. The entry into the water is quite gradual, but the depth increases faster than on shallow beaches. The water here is amazingly clean and has a deep blue hue.

Infrastructure: On the coast, there are several pensions, a couple of hotels, and family restaurants. On a hill above the eastern part of the bay sits the famous My Marina restaurant, offering excellent fresh fish dishes with a panoramic view of the entire harbor.

What to do
Beach holiday: Enjoy the cleanest sea and privacy.
Boat trips: Rent a motorboat for a trip to the caves of Ekincik or to the mouth of the Dalyan River.
Hiking: Walk along the paths through the pine forest, which offer stunning views of the sea.

Pros and Cons
Pros:
Incredible natural beauty, clean air, and crystal-clear sea.
Calm, respectable atmosphere without the noise of mass tourism.
Excellent starting point for a boat trip to Dalyan.
High-quality fish restaurants with beautiful views.

Cons:
The road to the bay from Köyceğiz is quite winding and steep.
The village's infrastructure is modest, with no large shopping centers or active nightlife.
Beach pebbles can be hot in the height of summer (special shoes are recommended).

Who is Ekincik suitable for?
Ekincik is ideal for yachtsmen, romantic couples, and road travelers looking for a quiet place with beautiful nature and quality cuisine. It will appeal to those who appreciate the aesthetics of privacy, the cleanliness of the sea, and slow evenings under the rustle of waves and the clinking of glasses.
