Köyceğiz is a charming town and district in the Muğla Province of Turkey, situated in a unique location where the Aegean Sea meets the Mediterranean. The town lies at the northern end of Köyceğiz Lake, which is connected to the Mediterranean Sea by a natural channel known as the Dalyan Delta.

### **Geographical Location and Nature**

Köyceğiz is famous for its unique ecosystem, which is protected as part of the Köyceğiz-Dalyan Special Environmental Protection Area. This area is home to rare species of flora and fauna, including loggerhead sea turtles (Caretta caretta) and blue crabs. The lake, surrounded by mountains and forests, is fed by three tributaries. Excess water flows through the 10-kilometer Dalyan channel into the Mediterranean Sea, creating a unique delta.

### **History**

The history of Köyceğiz dates back to ancient times. According to the historian Herodotus, the geographer Strabo, and the British archaeologist Hoskyn, settlements existed here as early as 3400 BC. The first inhabitants of these places were the Carians. Subsequently, the region was inhabited by Scythians, Assyrians, Ionians, Achaeans, Persians, Greeks, Seleucids, Romans, Seljuks, and Ottomans.

The ancient city of Kaunos, located near modern-day Dalyan, was an important trading port of the Carians and played a key role in the region's economy.

### **Climate**

Köyceğiz has a Mediterranean climate with hot summers (Csa under the Köppen classification). Summers are very hot and dry, while winters are mild and rainy. In fact, after Rize, Köyceğiz is the second wettest region in Turkey during the winter period.

### **Economy and Tourism**

The backbone of the Köyceğiz economy consists of agriculture, animal husbandry, forestry, and tourism. The region is famous for honey production, especially pine and citrus honey.

Tourists are attracted by the natural beauty, calm atmosphere, and historical sights. The main activities include:
- **Boat trips** on the lake and the Dalyan River.
- **Visiting mud baths** and the Sultaniye hot springs.
- **Excursions** to the ancient city of Kaunos.
- **Relaxing** on Iztuzu Beach.
- **Visiting the local market**, which opens on Mondays and is one of the largest in Turkey.

Köyceğiz is an ideal destination for those seeking a peaceful holiday in the heart of nature, away from the noise of major resorts, but with the opportunity to experience the rich history and culture of the region.
