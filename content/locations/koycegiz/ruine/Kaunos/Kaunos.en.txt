Kaunos — The forgotten port where history comes alive
When you sail by boat along the Dalyan River and admire the majestic Lycian tombs, you see only the necropolis — the facade of the huge and once-powerful city of Kaunos, which lies on the other side of the cliff. Visiting the city itself is an opportunity to look behind the facade and touch the daily life, rise, and decline of an ancient civilization.

The Rise and Fall of a Port City
Kaunos was an important port city of ancient Caria, located on the border with Lycia. Its prosperity was entirely connected with the sea and trade, especially salt, slaves, and salted fish.

However, what gave the city life eventually ruined it. Silt deposits from the Dalyan River gradually silted up its thriving harbor. Over time, the sea retreated, the port became shallow, and Kaunos lost its strategic importance. The inhabitants abandoned the city, and it was buried under the sands of time until archaeologists discovered it in the 19th century.

Walking through the ruins: what to see?
To get to Kaunos from Dalyan, you need to cross the river on a small rowing boat ferry and then walk for about 20 minutes — which is an authentic adventure in itself. On the territory of the archaeological park, you will find:

Theater: An impressive Greco-Roman theater carved into the hillside. It is well preserved and could accommodate about 5,000 spectators. The upper rows offer a beautiful view of the river and the surrounding area.

Roman Baths (Thermae) and Palaestra: A large complex demonstrating the Roman period of the city's history. One can easily imagine the scale of these public baths and the sports ground.

Agora (Market Square): The central square of the city, where trade was buzzing. Next to it, you can see a unique measuring bas-relief for salted fish.

Round Fountain House: A mysterious round structure in the center of the agora, the purpose of which is still debated.

Acropolis: On the top of the hill are the remains of the fortifications of the acropolis. Climbing there takes effort but is rewarded with the best panoramic view of the entire valley, the river, Iztuzu Beach, and the ruins of Kaunos itself.

Pros and Cons
Pros:
A large and interesting archaeological complex with many well-preserved structures.
Opportunity to understand the history of the tombs seen from the river.
Stunning views from the theater and the acropolis.
Far fewer tourists than in more famous ancient cities like Ephesus.

Cons:
Requires crossing the river and a walk.
It can be very hot in summer, and there is little shade on the territory.
The most famous part (the tombs) is visible only from a distance, from the river.

Who will Kaunos be of interest to?
Kaunos is a must-visit for anyone interested in history and archaeology. It is also a great addition to the standard Dalyan boat tour. If you have seen the tombs from the water and wondered, "What is there behind the cliff?", then Kaunos will give you a comprehensive answer. It is a journey to the "other side" of history, allowing you to see the full picture of the life of a great ancient city.
