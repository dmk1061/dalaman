# Sarsala Bay (Sarsala Koyu) — Picturesque Cove Surrounded by Pine Hills

Sarsala Bay is a scenic pebbly cove in Dalaman, surrounded by pine-covered hills, sheltered from waves, and highly popular among yachting enthusiasts.

### Beach and Bay Features
Sarsala Bay is naturally sheltered from strong winds and open seas by lush pine-covered hills. The water is exceptionally clean, calm, and warm, with virtually no large waves, making it perfect for swimming and snorkeling. The shoreline is mostly pebbles mixed with sand, and the depth increases rather quickly. A lifeguard station and beach swings are set up on the shore. On weekends, the beach can become very crowded with local visitors.

### Amenities and Road
The beach is managed by the [Dalaman](/en/dalaman) Municipality, which operates a budget-friendly cafe on-site. Visitors can use changing cabins, showers, toilets, and rent sunbeds and umbrellas. The road from [Dalaman](/en/dalaman) center is paved, though the final few kilometers turn into a narrow, winding mountain serpentine that requires careful driving.

### Practical Information
* **Location:** 17 km from [Dalaman](/en/dalaman) city center.
* **Road:** Asphalt (narrow and winding serpentine at the end).
* **Infrastructure:** Municipal cafe, toilets, showers, changing cabins, sunbeds and umbrellas, lifeguard station, parking.
* **Entrance:** Paid. A parking/entry fee per vehicle is charged (approx. 150–200 TL per day, covering parking and use of basic facilities).
* **Tip:** Wear water shoes as the entrance to the water is pebbly. During the peak summer season, arrive early to secure a parking spot. Note that official camping and lighting open fires or barbecues are strictly prohibited.
