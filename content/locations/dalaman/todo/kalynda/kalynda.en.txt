# Kalynda (Kalynda Antik Kenti) — Wild Ancient Ruins on a Hilltop

The ancient city of Kalynda is an untouched archaeological site on Asar-Tepe hill near Serefler, attracting lovers of wild hiking and historical trails.

### History and Ruins
Kalynda is a lesser-known ancient Carian city. Unlike the region's popular archaeological sites, it is completely undeveloped for mass tourism: there are no fences, paved walkways, ticket booths, or information signs. It remains a wild destination for ruin seekers. At the summit of Asar-Tepe, visitors can explore fragments of Hellenistic fortress walls and the outlines of ancient foundations. In the surrounding areas, rock-cut tombs can also be spotted. The hilltop offers a panoramic view over [Dalaman](/en/dalaman) Airport.

### Access
The ascent to the ruins goes through olive groves and pine forests. The road to Serefler village is paved, but the stretch leading to the foot of the hill is an unpaved dirt track that might be challenging for low-passenger cars. The final climb to the summit must be done on foot via a steep, stony trail.

### Practical Information
* **Location:** 4 km east of [Dalaman](/en/dalaman) (near Serefler village).
* **Road:** Paved road to Serefler village, then a rough dirt road to the hill's base, followed by a steep hike up a rocky path.
* **Infrastructure:** Wild archaeological site (zero infrastructure: no cafes, toilets, water, or signs).
* **Entrance:** Free.
* **Tip:** Wear sturdy hiking shoes with a good grip. Be sure to carry plenty of drinking water and wear a hat, as there is no shade or facilities at the ruins.
