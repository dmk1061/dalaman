# Alexandria Train Station (Dalaman Tren İstasyonu) — Station with No Rails

The Alexandria Train Station in Dalaman is a unique historical building built in 1905, located inside the TİGEM estate and famous for its curious legend.

### The Mix-Up Legend
According to popular local folklore, a grand mistake occurred during its construction. Abbas II Hilmi Pasha, the last Khedive of Egypt, ordered a railway station for Alexandria and a hunting lodge for his estate in [Dalaman](/en/dalaman) at the same time. The French cargo ships allegedly swapped the blueprints and building materials, delivering the train station project to Turkey and the hunting villa to Egypt. Although historians view this as a charming myth, the building was actually designed as a luxury residence in the fashionable European style of the era.

### Current Status
Today, this beautiful landmark serves as the administrative building for the State Agricultural Directorate (TİGEM). Since it is an active government office, the interior is closed to tourists. However, visitors can view and photograph the building from the outside and walk around the beautiful surrounding gardens adorned with towering palm trees.

### Practical Information
* **Location:** [Dalaman](/en/dalaman) city center (Enver Ozdemir Street, inside the TİGEM compound).
* **Road:** Asphalt.
* **Infrastructure:** None (active government administrative building).
* **Entrance:** Free. Access is limited to the outer gardens; entry to the building interior is closed, and security guards may limit access to active farm zones.
* **Tip:** Visit this spot to capture unique architectural photos. The historic facade framed by century-old palm trees offers a stunning backdrop.
