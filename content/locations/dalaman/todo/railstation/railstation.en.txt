Dalaman Railway Station — The Station Waiting for Its Train
In the heart of the State Agricultural Administration of Dalaman (Dalaman Tarım İşletmesi Müdürlüğü) stands a magnificent stone building that looks completely out of place among orange groves and palm trees. This building was built as a railway station, but ironically, Dalaman has never had, and still does not have, a railway.

The story of a mistake: Two projects, one shipment
The most popular and romantic version of this story is associated with the last Khedive (ruler) of Egypt, Abbas Hilmi Pasha. At the beginning of the 20th century, he was a passionate hunter and often came to the fertile Dalaman valley, where he had extensive estates.

According to legend, the Pasha ordered two projects at the same time to be delivered on ships from France:

A railway station for Alexandria in Egypt.

A hunting lodge (pavilion) for his estate in Dalaman.

Due to an unbelievable mistake, the ships mixed up the destinations. The materials for the luxury hunting lodge went to Egypt, and the materials for a full-fledged railway station with ticket offices, a waiting room, and administrative offices arrived in Dalaman. Despite the mistake, the Pasha ordered the station to be built, hoping in the future to still lay a railway line here to make it easier to go hunting. However, the First World War and subsequent political events ruined his plans.

What is here today?
The railway was never built. Today, this beautiful and perfectly preserved building is the administrative block of the State Agricultural Administration. It can be viewed from the outside while walking through the well-kept and green territory of the farm.

Pros and cons
Pros:

Incredibly interesting and unusual story.

Beautiful architecture, atypical for this region.

Opportunity to see a unique historical curiosity.

Located on the beautiful, well-kept territory of the state farm.

Cons:

You can only view the building from the outside.

It is not a full-fledged tourist site, but rather just a historical monument.

Who will be interested?
Visiting the "trainless station" is for curious travelers and lovers of unusual stories. This is a wonderful opportunity to turn off the tourist path and see something truly unique. If you are traveling by car in the Dalaman area and have a little free time, be sure to look into the territory of the state farm to see the loneliest station in the world with your own eyes.
