Dalaman is an excellent base for exploring the rich historical and natural heritage of southwestern Turkey. Although the city itself does not abound with tourist sites, its surroundings are full of unique places that are easily accessible.

### **Within Dalaman**

*   [🚣 <u>**Dalaman River (Dalaman Çayı)**</u>](/dalaman/sight/river): This mountain river is one of the best rafting spots in Turkey. Rapid sections of varying difficulty attract both beginners and experienced thrill-seekers. Rafting down the river is not only an adrenaline rush but also an opportunity to enjoy stunning canyon views.
*   [🏛️ <u>**Ancient City of Kalynda (Kalynda Antik Kenti)**</u>](/dalaman/sight/kalynda): Located near the center of Dalaman, the ruins of an ancient Carian city. Although excavations here have not been as active as in other places, you can see the remains of the city walls and the acropolis, allowing you to touch ancient history.
*   [🚂 <u>**Historical Railway Station**</u>](/dalaman/sight/railstation): A unique and somewhat curious building built at the beginning of the 20th century. The Egyptian ruler ordered it as a hunting lodge, but by mistake, the design and materials for a station from Alexandria were delivered here. So a railway station appeared in Dalaman, where there has never been a railway. Today it houses the state agricultural administration.
*   [♨️ <u>**Dalaman Sulfur Springs**</u>](/dalaman/sight/kukurt): A natural health resort with thermal water and healing muds. Springs with a high sulfur content are known for their healing properties for skin diseases and joint pain. An authentic place for healing far from tourist gloss.

### **Immediate Surroundings (Day Trips)**

*   **Dalyan:** Just a 30-minute drive from Dalaman is this unique town through which the river of the same name flows. A boat excursion is a must to see:
    *   **Lycian Rock Tombs:** Majestic royal tombs carved into the cliffs in the 4th century BC.
    *   **Ancient City of Kaunos:** The ruins of a once-flourishing port city with a well-preserved amphitheater, Roman baths, and an acropolis.
    *   **Mud Baths and Thermal Springs:** The famous Sultaniye springs, beneficial for skin and health.

*   **Göcek and the "12 Islands" Tour:** Göcek is a fashionable port town from where popular boat trips around the Gulf of Fethiye start. This is an opportunity to spend the whole day at sea, swimming in secluded turquoise coves.

*   **Tlos and Saklıkent:**
    *   **Tlos:** One of the oldest and most important cities of the Lycian League. Here you can see an impressive acropolis, rock tombs, a stadium, and a theater.
    *   **Saklıkent:** The deepest canyon in Turkey, 18 km long. Tourists can walk several kilometers deep into the canyon through the icy water of a mountain river, which is an unforgettable adventure on a hot day.
