# Asi Bay (Aşı Koyu) — A Secluded Turquoise Paradise Behind Pine Hills
A picturesque and secluded Asi Bay near Dalyan, famous for its turquoise water, a rough dirt road through pine forests, and a zipline over the sea.

Aşı Koyu (Asi Bay) is a rewarding destination for adventurous travelers. Hidden among pine-covered hills between Dalyan and Sarıgerme, this bay is well-sheltered from winds and open waves. The shoreline consists of small dark pebbles, which keeps the water crystal clear and gives it a stunning turquoise hue. A small rocky islet sits in the middle of the bay, making it a scenic spot for swimming, cliff jumping, and snorkeling.

The beach features a cafe-restaurant offering sunbed and umbrella rentals, toilets, showers, and changing cabins. A unique highlight of Aşı Koyu is its 150-meter-long zipline, suspended about 5 meters above the sea surface, allowing visitors to glide directly over the water. Mobile coverage and internet can be very weak in the valley, so it is highly recommended to bring cash Turkish Lira for payments on-site.

### Practical Information
- Location: 20 km south of Dalyan (approx. 40-50 minutes drive), 14 km from Sarıgerme
- Road: Asphalt road to Mergenli village, followed by a steep, narrow, and winding 5 km gravel/dirt road. Driving slowly and carefully is advised
- Infrastructure: Cafe-restaurant, toilets, showers, changing cabins, sunbed rentals, picnic area, zipline over the water
- Entrance: Vehicle entry fee applies (approx. 70 TL per car), pedestrian entry is free. Sunbeds, umbrellas, and zipline rides are paid extra
- Tip: Bring cash Turkish Lira, as card payment terminals often fail due to poor mobile reception. To avoid crowds, visit on a weekday morning. Water shoes are recommended for walking on the pebbly shore.
