Aşı Cove — A Hidden Paradise Behind Seven Hills
Aşı Cove is a reward for the persistent. Hidden among pine-covered hills, it is in no hurry to open up to every tourist. The road here is a small adventure in itself, but those who overcome it will be rewarded with views of incredible beauty, a crystal clear sea, and a feeling that they have found a secret place known to only a few.

Main feature: Isolation and crystal clear water
Unlike easily accessible beaches, Aşı has preserved its pristine atmosphere precisely because of its relative inaccessibility. The last few kilometers of the road to the cove are a winding and uneven descent that deters many.

The result is incredibly clean, transparent, and calm turquoise water. The cove is well protected from winds and waves, and a small rocky islet in the middle adds picturesqueness to the landscape. It is an ideal place for swimming and snorkeling — the underwater world here is very rich.

Beach and infrastructure
Coastline: The beach is small and covered with pebbles, so the water always remains clear.

Infrastructure: Despite its "wildness", there is a single işletme (private enterprise/restaurant) in the cove. Here you can rent sunbeds and umbrellas, have a bite to eat, or have a cold drink. There is also a shower and toilet. Prices are generally reasonable. However, you can also sit on your towel for free.

Atmosphere: Very calm and relaxed. Even at the height of the season, there is no feeling of crowds. This is a place for those who want to escape the noise and bustle.

How to get there
By car: The only way to get there by land. You need to drive from Dalyan or Sarigerme towards the village of Mergenli, and then follow the signs. The last stretch of the road (about 5 km) is unpaved and requires careful driving.

By boat: Aşı Cove is also a popular stop for boat excursions from Dalyan and Marmaris.
