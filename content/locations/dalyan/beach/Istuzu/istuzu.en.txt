# Iztuzu Turtle Beach (İztuzu Plajı) — A Protected Spit Between Sea and River
The unique Iztuzu Turtle Beach near Dalyan is a 4.5km sandy spit separating the warm Mediterranean Sea and the freshwater delta of the Dalyan River.

İztuzu Beach is a unique nature reserve. It is a narrow sandy strip serving as a natural border between the sea and the river delta. The beach's primary treasure is the rare loggerhead sea turtles (Caretta caretta), which have nested here from May to September for centuries. Consequently, strict ecological rules apply: visiting the beach is prohibited at night, pets are not allowed, and nesting sites are protected with metal cages.

The beach has two main entrance areas on opposite ends. One (the "road end") is accessible by driving along a scenic road past Lake [Sulungur](/en/dalyan/todo/sulungur). The other (the "boat end") is reachable only by water. The boat end hosts the Sea Turtle Research, Rescue, and Rehabilitation Center (DEKAMER), which is free to visit. Both ends of the beach are equipped with changing cabins, showers, toilets, and municipal cafes. The beach features soft golden sand and a very shallow entry into the water, making it excellent for families.

### Practical Information
- Location: 12 km south of Dalyan
- Road: Asphalt (by car or public dolmuş in about 20 minutes) to the eastern side; or by cooperative public taxi-boats from Dalyan harbor through the reed channels of the delta (about 40 minutes) to the western side
- Infrastructure: Municipal cafes, showers, toilets, changing cabins, lifeguard stations, sunbed and umbrella rentals, DEKAMER turtle rescue center (on the western end)
- Entrance: Paid (a small environmental fee is charged at the entrance), paid parking. Boat-dolmuş tickets are purchased separately
- Tip: The beach is strictly closed to the public from 20:00 to 08:00. We recommend taking the water taxi to enjoy the scenic boat trip through the delta, and make sure to visit the turtle rescue center.
