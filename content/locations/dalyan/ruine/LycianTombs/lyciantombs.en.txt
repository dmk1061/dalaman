# Kaunos Tombs (Kaunos Kral Mezarları) — Rock-Cut King Tombs in Dalyan
The majestic 4th-century BC rock tombs of Kaunos kings, carved into a sheer cliff on the bank of the Dalyan River, serving as the region's symbol.

The royal rock tombs of ancient Kaunos, towering over the river opposite the town of [Dalyan](/en/dalyan), represent one of Turkey's most spectacular archaeological wonders. Carved into the steep cliffside over 2400 years ago (4th century BC), these monumental tombs were designed for the rulers of the ancient city of Kaunos. The facades of the tombs mimic Greek temples, complete with columns and pediments. The Lycians believed that winged creatures carried the souls of the dead to heaven, which is why they constructed their tombs as high as possible.

To protect them from erosion and vandalism, climbing up to the tombs is strictly prohibited. However, they are perfectly visible from river boats and the Dalyan town waterfront. To get a closer look, you can cross the river in a traditional rowboat and walk along the dirt path at the base of the cliffs. This path also leads to the ruins of the ancient city of Kaunos, which features an ancient theater, Roman baths, and a Byzantine basilica. The tombs are beautifully illuminated at night.

### Practical Information
- Location: Opposite the town of [Dalyan](/en/dalyan), on the western bank of the Dalyan River
- Road: Boat (rowboat crossing from Dalyan harbor for a small fee), followed by a 15-minute walk along a dirt path under the cliffs to the Kaunos ruins
- Infrastructure: Viewpoint on the Dalyan promenade, evening lighting. On the opposite bank, there are ticket booths for the Kaunos archaeological site and toilets
- Entrance: Viewing the tombs from the Dalyan waterfront is free. Rowboat crossing is paid (approx. 30-50 TL). Entrance to the Kaunos ruins is paid (approx. 3 EUR / 100-110 TL, Turkish Museum Card / Müze Kart is valid)
- Tip: The most beautiful views of the tombs are from the water during a river boat tour through the Dalyan delta, or at sunset from the city promenade when the floodlights are turned on.
