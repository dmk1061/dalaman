Lycian Tombs — Gates to Eternity on Rocky Shores
Lycian tombs are not just graves, but a monumental testament to the unique culture and beliefs of the ancient Lycian people who inhabited the southwestern coast of modern Turkey. These impressive structures, carved directly into sheer cliffs, are one of the most prominent symbols of the region.

Why in the rocks?
The Lycians believed that after death, the souls of the deceased were carried to the afterlife by winged creatures resembling sirens. Therefore, to ease the soul's journey to heaven, they built their "houses of eternity" as high as possible, closer to the sky. Such a high location also emphasized the status and wealth of the people buried in them — the higher the tomb, the more noble its owner was.

Architecture
Many tombs mimic the facades of Lycian residential houses or Greek temples with columns, porticoes, and pediments. This skillful stone carving, performed thousands of years ago without modern tools, is amazing in its precision and detail.

Who will be interested?
Visiting the Lycian Tombs is a must for anyone interested in history, archaeology, and ancient civilizations.

In Fethiye, you can approach the tombs closely, appreciate the scale of the work, and enjoy the panoramic view.

In Dalyan, you will get an unforgettable experience of contemplating these majestic structures from the water, which creates a very special, almost mystical atmosphere.

In any case, the Lycian Tombs are a powerful reminder of the rich historical past of this land, leaving a deep mark on the memory of every traveler.
