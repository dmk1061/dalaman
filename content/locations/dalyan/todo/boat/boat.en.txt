Ekincik — Haven for Yachtsmen and Gateway to Dalyan
Ekincik Cove is a magnificent, wide and deep bay surrounded by dense pine forests descending directly to the water. It has long earned a reputation as one of the most beautiful and safest "havens" for yachts and "blue cruises" on this coast. But Ekincik is not only an anchorage for sailors, but also a self-sufficient place for a quiet holiday in nature.

Main feature: Natural harbor and proximity to Dalyan
Ideal anchorage for yachts: Thanks to its shape and depth, the cove is excellently protected from winds and waves, making it a favorite place for yachtsmen to spend the night. There is a small marina and several restaurants with their own piers.

Gateway to Dalyan: Unique excursions to Dalyan are organized from Ekincik. Since large yachts cannot enter the shallow river delta, tourists transfer from them to small local "piyade" boats right in Ekincik Cove and travel on them to the Lycian Tombs, mud baths and Iztuzu Beach.

Beach and atmosphere
Coastline: There is a long beach in the cove, consisting of a mix of sand and pebbles. The sea here is very clean and calm.

Atmosphere: Very quiet, relaxed, with a "maritime" feel. Life here flows slowly and is concentrated around the marina and several coastal hotels and campsites. This place has preserved its naturalness and is not spoiled by mass development.

Infrastructure: Quite modest. There are several hotels, pensions, restaurants and popular campsites. This is a place for those looking for connection with nature, not noisy entertainment.

How to get there
By sea: The most popular way. Ekincik is a mandatory stop on many "blue cruise" routes.

By land: The road from Köyceğiz (about 35 km) is very scenic, but, like many secluded coves, rather winding and mountainous. The views of Lake Köyceğiz along the way are simply stunning.

Pros and cons
Pros:

Stunning natural beauty and a sense of pristine nature.

Very clean and calm sea.

Ideal place for yachting and camping enthusiasts.

Unique opportunity to go on an excursion to Dalyan directly from the cove.

Cons:

Difficult road by land.

Very limited infrastructure and complete lack of nightlife.

More focused on "maritime" travelers than on ordinary tourists.

Who is Ekincik suitable for?
Ekincik is a paradise for yachtsmen, lovers of wild nature, camping and caravanning. It is an ideal choice for those traveling by sea and looking for a beautiful and safe anchorage. Also, this place will appeal to those who want to relax away from civilization, in a tent or a simple pension, surrounded by a pine forest. If you are looking for peace, a very clean sea and are not afraid of winding roads, Ekincik will be a real discovery for you.
