# Dalyan River Boat Tour (Dalyan Tekne Turu) — Journey Through the Reed Labyrinth
A popular boat tour along the Dalyan River past the ancient rock tombs of Kaunos to the therapeutic mud baths and protected Iztuzu Turtle Beach.

A river cruise on a flat-bottomed wooden motorboat (piyade) is the signature experience of [Dalyan](/en/dalyan) and the best way to explore the region's main attractions in a single day. The route winds through the natural channels of the Dalyan River delta, surrounded by towering reeds that create a green labyrinth. During the trip, boatmen point out the unique wildlife of the delta, including Nile softshell turtles and numerous bird species.

The classic day-trip itinerary includes several essential stops. The boat pauses directly beneath the majestic [Kaunos Rock Tombs](/en/dalyan/ruine/LycianTombs) for a photo opportunity. Next, visitors stop at the therapeutic thermal mud baths (either at the Sultaniye Spa or local riverside pools) to cover themselves in mineral-rich mud and soak in sulfur-rich hot springs. The tour concludes where the delta meets the open sea at the protected [Iztuzu Beach](/en/dalyan/beach/Istuzu).

### Practical Information
- Location: Riverfront piers in the center of [Dalyan](/en/dalyan)
- Road: Water transport (wooden motorized riverboats)
- Infrastructure: Sun-canopies on boats, life jackets. Facilities (toilets, showers, cafes) are available at the stops (mud baths, Iztuzu Beach)
- Entrance: Group tours cost around 25–40 EUR (often including lunch). Private boat rentals from the local cooperative in Dalyan center cost around 1500–3000 TL depending on the duration and route. Entrance fees to the mud baths (approx. 50-100 TL) are often paid separately
- Tip: Bring a swimsuit (preferably dark-colored, as the mineral mud can stain light fabrics), a towel, sunscreen, and a hat. It can be breezy on the river, so a light jacket or cover-up is useful.
