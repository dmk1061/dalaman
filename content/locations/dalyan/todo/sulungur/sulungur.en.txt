# Lake Sülüngür (Sülüngür Gölü) — Dalyan's Mirror and Sunset Haven

Lake Sülüngür is a scenic brackish lake near Dalyan, famous for its mirror-like waters, pine-forested hills, birdwatching, and breathtaking golden sunsets.

This quiet and mesmerizing body of water lies right on the way to the famous [Iztuzu Beach](/en/dalyan/beach/istuzu). As a vital part of the unique [Dalyan](/en/dalyan) River delta ecosystem, Lake Sülüngür was formed when river silt gradually sealed off a former marine bay from the open sea.

The main charm of this place is absolute tranquility. Unlike the dynamic coastline, silence reigns supreme here. Its smooth water surface reflects fluffy clouds and pine-clad hills. The lake is particularly beautiful during the pre-sunset hours, when the sky and water are painted in delicate shades of pink, orange, and purple.

### Things to Do
* **Birdwatching:** The lake is an important migratory stop and home to dozens of bird species. Bring binoculars.
* **Sunset Photography:** This is a favorite spot for local landscape photographers during the "golden hour".
* **Walking and Cycling:** A paved road with scenic views and several dirt paths run along the lake.
* **Lakeside Picnic:** Wooden tables and benches are set up along the shore for resting and dining.

### Restrictions
The lake is managed by the local fisheries cooperative, DALKO, which breeds grey mullet and sea bass here. Consequently, swimming in the lake is officially prohibited, and amateur fishing from the shore is strictly restricted. Camping and setting up tents on the shores are also not allowed due to the area's protected nature status.

### Practical Information
* **Location:** 5 km from Dalyan town center, along the road to Iztuzu Beach.
* **Road:** Excellent smooth asphalt road, suitable for any vehicle, bicycle, or dolmuş ride.
* **Infrastructure:** Viewpoints, benches, a couple of cozy cafes (such as Sülüngür Kafe), and a fish restaurant. No showers or beach amenities.
* **Entrance:** Free. Roadside parking is free.
* **Tip:** Schedule your visit for about 30–40 minutes before sunset, and make sure to bring mosquito repellent.
