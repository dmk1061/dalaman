# Radar Hill (Radar Tepesi) — The Best Panoramic Viewpoint Over Dalyan

Radar Hill near Dalyan is the region's ultimate viewpoint, offering 360-degree vistas over Iztuzu Beach, local lakes, and the winding river delta.

Radar Hill (Radar Tepesi), which gets its name from the radio transmitter tower at its summit, is the highest observation point in the vicinity of [Dalyan](/en/dalyan). From this bird's-eye perspective, a fantastic 360-degree circular panorama of the entire Dalyan River delta unfolds. The winding reed channels, Lake [Sülüngür](/en/dalyan/todo/sulungur), the endless Mediterranean Sea, and the 4.5-kilometer sandy spit of [Iztuzu Beach](/en/dalyan/beach/Istuzu) are all visible in detail. On a clear day, you can even spot Lake Köyceğiz on the horizon.

There is no tourist infrastructure at the top — only wild nature and breathtaking views. The road to the summit is a significant challenge: while the first part is paved, the final ascent consists of a steep, narrow, and rocky dirt road with deep ruts. Reaching the peak in a standard passenger car is highly difficult and unsafe for the suspension. Therefore, visitors use 4x4 off-road vehicles, quad bikes, or hike to the top after parking below. Organized sunset jeep tours are also highly popular.

### Practical Information
- Location: 15 km south of [Dalyan](/en/dalyan), above Gökbel village.
- Road: Asphalt until the start of the climb, then the last few kilometers are a very steep, rocky, and rough dirt track. A 4x4 vehicle, ATV, or hiking is recommended.
- Infrastructure: None (wild viewpoint, communication tower).
- Entrance: Free, open parking (wild).
- Tip: Arrive at the hill about an hour before sunset, when the setting sun paints the delta and the sea in golden and crimson tones. Be sure to bring water and a windbreaker, as it is almost always windy on the open summit.
