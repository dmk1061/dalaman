## Main Sights of Dalyan: Where History Meets Nature

Dalyan is a place where amazing discoveries await you at every step. Here, ancient history harmoniously intertwines with unique natural landscapes. Most sights are accessible during a [⛵ <u>**relaxing boat trip**</u>](/dalyan/sight/boat) along the river.

### 1. [🏛️ <u>**Lycian Tombs (Lycian Rock Tombs)**</u>](/dalyan/sight/LycianTombs)
This is Dalyan's signature landmark. The majestic tombs, carved into sheer cliffs in the 4th century BC, were intended for the rulers and nobility of ancient Lycia. They look especially impressive at sunset and with evening illumination. You can see them only from the river during a boat tour — it is forbidden to approach the tombs themselves, which helps preserve this unique monument.

### 2. Ancient City of Kaunos (Kaunos)
On the opposite bank of the river from Dalyan are the ruins of Kaunos — a once-flourishing ancient port city. To get here, you need to cross the river in a small boat and then walk a bit. On the territory of Kaunos, you will see:
- **The Acropolis**, which offers a stunning view of the river, delta, and Iztuzu Beach.
- **A well-preserved amphitheater**, designed for 5,000 spectators.
- **Roman baths, the agora, temples, and remains of the city walls.**

A walk through Kaunos is a real immersion in history.

### 3. Iztuzu Beach and the Turtle Rehabilitation Center (DEKAMER)
A trip to Dalyan will not be complete without visiting the famous \"Turtle Beach\". In addition to the unique opportunity to swim in both the salty sea and fresh lake, it is worth visiting:
- **DEKAMER (Sea Turtle Research, Rescue and Rehabilitation Center):** Located right by the beach, this center is dedicated to saving and treating injured sea turtles. By visiting the center, you can see these amazing creatures up close and make a donation for their protection.

### 4. [♨️ <u>**Mud Baths and Sulfur Springs**</u>](/dalyan/sight/sulungur)
One of the most popular \"entertainment sights\" in Dalyan. It is believed that local muds and thermal waters, rich in sulfur, have healing and rejuvenating properties. Tourists enjoy coating themselves in mud, letting it dry in the sun, and then washing it off in the thermal pool. The most famous complex is located on the shore of Lake Köyceğiz.

### 5. Lake Köyceğiz (Köyceğiz Gölü)
A huge freshwater lake connected to the sea by the Dalyan River. Boat tours often include a stroll on the lake, where you can swim in its calm waters and admire the picturesque landscapes.

### 6. [📡 <u>**Radar Hill**</u>](/dalyan/sight/radar)
For those traveling by car, a trip to the viewing platform near the old radar station is a must. From here, the most breathtaking panoramic view of the entire Dalyan River delta and Iztuzu Beach opens up. This is the perfect spot for photos, especially at sunset.
