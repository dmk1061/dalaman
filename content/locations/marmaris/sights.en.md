## Main Sights of Marmaris and Surroundings

Marmaris is not only about great beaches and vibrant nightlife, but also a region rich in history and unique natural wonders. Here, every traveler will find something interesting, from ancient ruins to breathtaking landscapes.

### Historical and Cultural Sites

#### 1. [🏰 **<u>Marmaris Castle and Archaeological Museum (Marmaris Castle)</u>**](/marmaris/sight/castle)
Towering over the Old Town and the marina, the castle is the main historical landmark of Marmaris. Built by the Ionians and significantly rebuilt by the Ottoman Sultan Suleiman the Magnificent in the 16th century, it served as an important outpost. Today, the castle walls house an archaeological museum displaying artifacts from the Hellenistic, Roman, and Byzantine periods. The fortress walls offer the best panoramic view of the town and the bay.

#### 2. [🏘️ **<u>Old Town</u>**](/marmaris/sight/oldtown)
Stroll through the maze of narrow paved streets at the foot of the castle. Here you will find charming stone houses covered with flowers, small boutiques, souvenir shops, and cozy cafes. The atmosphere of the Old Town will transport you to the times of the Ottoman Empire.

#### 3. [🕌 **<u>Hafsa Sultan Caravanserai</u>**](/marmaris/sight/caravanserai)
This historical inn, built in 1545, once served as a haven for merchants and travelers. Today, its arched galleries house souvenir shops and cafes. It is a great place to feel the spirit of antiquity.

### Natural Sights

#### 4. [🌲 **<u>Marmaris National Park</u>**](/marmaris/sight/nationalpark)
A vast territory covered with pine forests surrounds the town and offers excellent opportunities for hiking, mountain biking, and jeep safaris. Here you can meet rare plant species, including endemic sweetgum trees (Liquidambar), and enjoy the silence away from the city hustle.

#### 5. [🕳️ **<u>Nimara Cave</u>**](/marmaris/sight/nimaracave)
Located on Heaven Island (Cennet Adası), this cave is an important archaeological site. Discoveries indicate that it was used as a place of worship as early as 12,000 years ago. The path to the cave goes along a picturesque forest trail.

### Excursions from Marmaris

#### 6. [♨️ **<u>Pamukkale</u>**](/marmaris/sight/pamukkale)
The "Cotton Castle" is one of the most recognizable natural wonders of Turkey and a UNESCO World Heritage site. It is a cascade of snow-white terraces and pools formed by calcium-rich thermal waters. Nearby are the ruins of the ancient city of Hierapolis. A trip to Pamukkale from Marmaris takes a whole day, but the impressions are worth it.

#### 7. [🏛️ **<u>Ancient City of Ephesus (Ephesus)</u>**](/marmaris/sight/ephesus)
One of the best-preserved ancient cities in the world. Walk along its marble streets, admire the Library of Celsus, the large amphitheater, and the ruins of the Temple of Artemis, one of the seven wonders of the ancient world.

#### 8. [🐢 **<u>Dalyan River & Iztuzu Beach</u>**](/marmaris/sight/dalyan)
A boat excursion along the Dalyan River is a journey past the impressive Lycian rock tombs carved into the cliffs. The tour includes a visit to the therapeutic mud springs and ends on Iztuzu Beach, which is a nesting site for the rare Caretta caretta sea turtles.
