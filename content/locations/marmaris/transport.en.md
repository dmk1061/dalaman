## Marmaris Transportation System: A Guide for Tourists

Getting around Marmaris and its picturesque surroundings is quite simple and convenient thanks to a well-developed transport system. Here are the main ways to help you explore the region.

### 1. Dolmuşes (Shared Minibuses)

The dolmuş is the most popular, convenient, and budget-friendly way to travel around the town and between neighboring resorts. These shared minibuses run on specific routes from early morning until late at night.

- **How to use:** To stop a dolmuş, just raise your hand anywhere along its route. To get off, tell the driver "inecek var," which means "someone is getting off."
- **Color Coding:** Dolmuş routes in Marmaris are color-coded. For example, green, orange, and blue ones run along the main route along the promenade between the center (Marmaris) and the Siteler district. Other colors lead to the Armutalan and Beldibi areas, etc.
- **Route to İçmeler:** Orange dolmuşes go to the neighboring resort village of İçmeler.
- **Payment:** Payment is made in cash to the driver upon boarding. The fare is fixed and indicated on a sign inside the vehicle.

### 2. Taxis

Taxis in Marmaris are easy to recognize by their bright yellow color. This is a more comfortable but also more expensive way of moving around, especially for short distances.

- **Stands and hailing:** Taxi stands (Taksi) are located at all busy spots in the town: near large hotels, on the promenade, and near the market. You can also call a taxi by phone or using special buttons installed on poles in many places.
- **Meter:** All official taxis are equipped with meters. Make sure the driver turns it on at the beginning of the trip.

### 3. Water Taxi

For traveling between Marmaris, İçmeler, and Turunç, there is a scenic alternative — the water taxi. These are small boats that run on schedule during the tourist season.

- **Routes:** The main routes connect the central marina of Marmaris with the waterfronts of İçmeler and Turunç.
- **Advantages:** A water taxi ride is not just a way to get from point A to point B, but a real boat trip with magnificent views of the coast.
- **Cost:** Tickets are more expensive than for the dolmuş, but the impressions are worth it.

### 4. Transport Rental

For those who prefer complete freedom of movement, there are many rental agencies in Marmaris.

- **Cars:** Renting a car is a great option for exploring distant surroundings like the Datça Peninsula or ancient cities. You will need an international driver's license to rent.
- **Scooters:** A scooter is the ideal transport for moving around Marmaris itself and nearby bays. It allows you to avoid traffic jams and does not require much parking space.
- **Bicycles:** An excellent bicycle path runs along the promenade of Marmaris and İçmeler. Renting a bike is a wonderful way to combine a stroll with sports.

### Intercity Buses

For trips to other Turkish cities, such as Fethiye, Bodrum, or Dalaman (where the nearest airport is located), you should head to the main Marmaris Bus Terminal (Otogar), located on the outskirts of the town. Comfortable buses from many major companies depart from here.
