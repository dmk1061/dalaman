# Turunç Beach (Turunç Plajı) — Cozy Blue Flag Bay in Marmaris

Turunç Beach is a peaceful, Blue Flag-certified resort cove located 20 km from Marmaris, offering a relaxed fishing village charm and calm, clear waters.

Surrounded by steep pine-covered mountains, this scenic destination retains the atmosphere of a traditional coastal village with low-rise buildings.

### Blue Flag Bay and Village Charm
The crescent-shaped beach of Turunç is composed of a mix of sand and fine pebbles. The bay is deeply indented into the coastline, which naturally protects it from open-sea waves and winds, making the water exceptionally calm and suitable for family swimming. The prestigious Blue Flag award guarantees excellent water quality and environmental management.

The village is renowned for its laid-back and quiet atmosphere. You will not find high-rise hotel blocks or noisy nightclubs here. The waterfront features a pleasant promenade lined with local fish restaurants, cafes, and souvenir shops. On Mondays, the local market in Turunç opens, offering fresh fruits, cheeses, and olive oils. Small boats depart regularly from the local harbor to neighboring Amos Bay and Kumlubük Beach.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** Bozburun Peninsula, [Marmaris](/en/marmaris) district, about 20 km southwest of the city center
- **Road:** Asphalt (winding, steep mountain road from Marmaris). Frequent dolmuşes (minibuses) connect Marmaris and Turunç (taking about 30–40 minutes). In the summer season, regular water taxis (shuttle boats) run between Marmaris harbor and Turunç pier.
- **Infrastructure:** Restrooms, showers, changing cabins, and lifeguard services are available. Sunbed and umbrella rentals are offered along the beach. Diving centers and water sports rental points are on-site.
- **Entrance:** Free (public access to the beach)
- **Tip:** Take a water taxi from Marmaris instead of driving. It is a scenic boat trip along the rocky Bozburun coastline that spares you from navigating the challenging, winding mountain pass.
