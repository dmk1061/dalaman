# Amos Bay (Amos Koyu) — Secluded Beach and Ancient Ruins Above the Sea

Amos Bay is a quiet pebble cove with crystal-clear waters, topped by the scenic, free-to-visit ruins of the ancient Lycian-Rhodian city of Amos.

It is an ideal destination for travelers who want to combine a relaxing beach day in a picturesque bay with a historical hike.

### Ancient City on the Hill and the Beach
The main attraction of Amos is the archaeological site situated on a high headland overlooking the bay. The highlight is the ancient theater, which once sat about 1,300 spectators. Sitting on its stone steps under the shade of pine trees and listening to the cicadas, you can enjoy a breathtaking panoramic view of the bay and the open Mediterranean. The remains of Hellenistic city walls and the ancient necropolis are also visible on the hill.

The beach itself is small and covered in fine pebbles. The sea here is exceptionally clear with a beautiful turquoise hue, making it a fantastic spot for snorkeling. There is no loud music or water sports, preserving a quiet, laid-back atmosphere.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** Bozburun Peninsula, about 24 km south of [Marmaris](/en/marmaris) and 4 km from Turunç village
- **Road:** Asphalt (narrow, winding mountain roads from Marmaris via Içmeler and Turunç). A small parking area is available near the ruins entrance. Alternatively, you can walk 30–40 minutes from Turunç or take a taxi.
- **Infrastructure:** The beach features the Amos Restaurant, which offers sunbed and umbrella rentals, restrooms, and showers. There are no facilities at the ancient ruins.
- **Entrance:** Free (no admission fee for either the beach or the ancient city ruins)
- **Tip:** Wear sturdy walking shoes or sneakers to climb the steep, rocky path to the theater. Bring drinking water and sun protection, as there is very little shade at the archaeological site.
