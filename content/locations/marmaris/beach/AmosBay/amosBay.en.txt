Amos Bay — Ancient peace under the shade of olive groves
If you are tired of the hustle and bustle of Marmaris, the loud music of central beaches, and the crowds of tourists, Amos Bay is your escape. Located just 24 kilometers from Marmaris, between the villages of Turunç and Kumlubük, this small and cozy bay offers a completely different format of recreation: silence, crystal-clear water, and a unique touch of history.

Main Feature: Pristine water and ancient ruins
Pristine Water: Amos Bay is famous for its clean water. It is one of the best places in the region for snorkeling. The water here is so transparent that even without a mask, you can see pebbles on the bottom and schools of small fish swimming around.

History Next Door: On the hill surrounding the bay lie the ruins of the ancient city of Amos, which dates back to the Hellenistic period. You can combine a beach holiday with a walk among the ancient ruins: see the remains of the amphitheater, temple foundations, and city walls. The climb to the ruins takes only 10-15 minutes, and from the top of the hill, a breathtaking panoramic view of the entire bay and the sea opens up.

Olive Groves: The bay is surrounded by ancient olive trees. Their silver-green leaves create a picturesque contrast with the turquoise sea.

Beach and Infrastructure
Coastline: The beach in Amos Bay is small and covered with large pebbles, so special swimming shoes are recommended. Thanks to the pebbles, the water remains crystal clear even during small waves.

Infrastructure: The beach is relatively wild, but there is a local restaurant, Amos, which rents out sun loungers and umbrellas. Here you can also have lunch with fresh fish and traditional Turkish mezes. There are changing rooms and showers on the territory.

What to do
Snorkeling: Bring a mask and snorkel — the underwater world of the bay is very active.

Walking to ancient Amos: Climb the hill for views and history.

Contemplative relaxation: This is an ideal place to read a book in silence or just listen to the rustle of waves.

Pros and Cons
Pros:
Crystal-clear water, ideal for snorkeling.
Very calm, quiet, and romantic place.
Opportunity to visit the ruins of an ancient city for free.
Beautiful nature and shade from olive trees.

Cons:
The beach is pebbly, which may not be comfortable for everyone.
No active water sports or entertainment.
Limited infrastructure (only one restaurant).
A car or water taxi is required to get there (public transport is rare).

Who is Amos Bay suitable for?
Amos Bay is ideal for couples, independent travelers, snorkeling enthusiasts, and everyone who appreciates silence, history, and pristine nature. It will appeal to those who prefer natural beauty and historic ruins to noisy clubs and slides.
