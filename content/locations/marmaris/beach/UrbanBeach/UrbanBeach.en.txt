# Marmaris Urban Beach (Marmaris Halk Plajı) — The Epicenter of Resort Life

Marmaris Urban Beach is a long sandy-pebble coastline located in the heart of the city, famous for its free public access and lively promenade vibe.

Stretching along the bustling Atatürk Boulevard, this beach attracts visitors with its ultimate accessibility, abundant entertainment, and proximity to major hotels.

### Rich Infrastructure and the Seaside Promenade
The coastline of the urban beach is relatively narrow, consisting of a mix of dark sand and fine pebbles. The Bay of Marmaris is naturally sheltered from the open sea by Nimara Peninsula and local islands, keeping the water calm and wave-free with a gradual slope. The beach regularly receives the Blue Flag award for water quality and safety standards.

The beach is divided into municipal (completely free) zones and private commercial sectors managed by seafront cafes and hotels. The municipal sections feature free public umbrellas, changing cabins, showers, and toilets maintained by the local municipality. An 8 km pedestrian promenade runs along the beach, linking central Marmaris to İçmeler. The promenade is lined with restaurants, bars, shops, and water sports stations offering jet-skiing and parasailing.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** Center of [Marmaris](/en/marmaris), along Atatürk Boulevard (Atatürk Caddesi)
- **Road:** Paved city streets. Easy access on foot from most central hotels. All local dolmuşes (minibuses) connecting Siteler, İçmeler, and the main bus station stop close to the beach.
- **Infrastructure:** Free municipal restrooms, showers, changing cabins, and lifeguards. Sunbeds can be rented, and free public umbrellas are set up in the municipal zones. Water sports centers are available.
- **Entrance:** Free (no entrance fee, open public access 24/7)
- **Tip:** To save money on sunbed rentals, head to the free municipal section (near the Atatürk statue), where you can spread your towel under public umbrellas. For a cleaner and slightly quieter swim within the city limits, walk towards the Siteler district, where the beach is wider.
