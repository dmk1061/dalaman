# Çiftebük Bay (Çiftebük Koyu) — A Wild Beach on the Bozburun Peninsula

Çiftebük Bay is a wild, twin-cove bay on the Bozburun Peninsula, famous for its untouched nature, turquoise waters, and complete lack of road access.

Tucked away from mainstream tourism, this hidden gem consists of two symmetrical coves divided by a narrow rocky spit, attracting yacht travelers seeking ultimate privacy.

### Untouched Nature and Isolation
Çiftebük is the epitome of a secret beach. There are no roads leading here, and reaching it by land involves hiking along steep, challenging mountain paths that require proper trekking footwear and a good level of fitness. The primary way to visit the bay is by sea, either on a private yacht charter or via boat tours departing from nearby villages like Bozburun, Söğüt, or Selimiye.

The shoreline is made of small and large pebbles, so water shoes are recommended. The sea is crystal clear, calm, and has a deep turquoise color, offering excellent conditions for snorkeling. The surrounding hills are covered with pine forests and wild olive trees, filling the air with a fresh herbal scent. There are no facilities of any kind on the beach.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** Bozburun Peninsula, [Marmaris](/en/marmaris) district, between Bozburun and Söğüt villages
- **Road:** Boat access only (yachts, speedboats) or via challenging wild hiking trails. No road access for cars.
- **Infrastructure:** None (wild beach). No cafes, toilets, fresh water, sunbeds, or reliable mobile reception.
- **Entrance:** Free
- **Tip:** If hiking to the bay, bring plenty of drinking water, snacks, sunscreen, and wear sturdy hiking boots. If arriving by boat, use water shoes to comfortably walk on the pebbly shore. Please pack out all your trash.
