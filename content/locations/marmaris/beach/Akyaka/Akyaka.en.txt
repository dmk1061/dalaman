# Akyaka Beach (Akyaka Plajı) — Kitesurfing Hub and the Icy Azmak River

Akyaka Beach is a spacious sandy coastline in the Gulf of Gökova, famous worldwide as a premier kitesurfing destination and for the icy Azmak River.

Situated at the head of the scenic Gulf of Gökova, this resort village attracts water sports enthusiasts, nature lovers, and families seeking a unique coastal getaway.

### Public Beach and the Kite Zone
Akyaka's coastline features two distinct sections:
1. **Akyaka Public Beach (Akyaka Halk Plajı):** A sandy beach with exceptionally shallow and calm waters stretching far out, making it safe and highly popular for families with young children.
2. **Akyaka Kite Beach:** Located slightly east of the town. Strong, steady thermal winds blow daily from May to October, combined with sandy shallows, making it one of the safest and best spots in Europe to learn and practice kitesurfing. Numerous kite schools and rental shops line the shore.

A major natural attraction is the cold, spring-fed Azmak River (Azmak Nehri), which runs along the town and empties into the sea. The river water is crystal clear and remains a chilly +8–10°C year-round. Diving into the icy river after swimming in the warm sea is a popular local tradition. Boat trips along the river offer views of a vibrant underwater habitat.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** Ula district, about 30 km north of [Marmaris](/en/marmaris), in the Gulf of Gökova
- **Road:** Asphalt (accessible via a winding but well-maintained mountain road from Marmaris). Frequent minibuses (dolmuş) connect Marmaris and Akyaka.
- **Infrastructure:** The public beach has sunbeds, umbrellas, showers, changing cabins, toilets, and nearby cafes. The kite beach has training schools, equipment rentals, showers, and bars.
- **Entrance:** Free entry to the public beach (sunbeds and umbrellas are paid). The dedicated Kite Beach may charge an entrance fee to cover facility maintenance for non-students.
- **Tip:** Take a scenic boat tour on the Azmak River to view the "natural aquarium" at the riverbed, followed by a meal at one of the riverside restaurants built on wooden decks directly over the flowing water.
