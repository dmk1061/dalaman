Cleopatra Beach (Sedir Island) — Golden sands of the Egyptian queen
This is one of the most famous, unique, and protected beaches in all of Turkey. Located on Sedir Island in the Gulf of Gökova, about 18 kilometers north of Marmaris, this beach is famous not only for its beauty but also for a unique natural phenomenon and a romantic ancient legend.

Main Feature: Unique sand and ancient legend
The Legend: According to legend, Roman general Mark Antony gave this island to his beloved Queen Cleopatra. However, she found the local sand unsuitable for her feet. Then, by Antony's order, special white sand was brought on ships from North Africa (presumably from Egypt) to create a perfect beach for the queen.

Unique Sand: Scientific analysis shows that the sand here is indeed unique. It consists of tiny spherical calcareous oolites, which are formed only under very specific conditions. The sand grains are round, do not stick to the feet, do not heat up in the sun, and look like miniature pearls. The sand is strictly protected by the government. It is forbidden to enter the beach in shoes, bring towels, or take even a handful of sand as a souvenir. Before leaving the beach, visitors must take a shower to wash off any remaining sand.

Turquoise lagoon: The water in the bay has an incredible, almost fluorescent turquoise-blue color, which, combined with the white sand, creates the feeling of a tropical paradise.

Beach and Infrastructure
Coastline: The beach area is small and fenced off to prevent sand loss. Visitors swim in a designated area. The entry into the water is very gradual and sandy.

Infrastructure: Since the island is an open-air museum, all infrastructure is municipal and strictly regulated. There are changing cabins, showers, toilets, and a small cafe. Sun loungers and umbrellas are included in the price of the entrance ticket to the archaeological site.

Ancient ruins: In addition to the beach, on the island you can see the ruins of the ancient city of Kedrai: a well-preserved theater, city walls, and ruins of a temple of Apollo.

How to get there
Only by water: You need to drive to the pier in the village of Çamlı (about 20 minutes from Marmaris) and then take a regular shuttle boat to the island (takes about 20 minutes). Also, trips to the island are part of many boat tours from Marmaris.

Pros and Cons
Pros:
Absolutely unique sand and a beautiful turquoise lagoon.
Opportunity to combine a beach holiday with visiting ancient ruins.
Well-kept and clean territory.
Romantic atmosphere and beautiful scenery.

Cons:
Paid entrance (since it is a state archaeological site; Museum Card is valid).
Can be extremely crowded in the height of the season.
Strict rules (you cannot lie directly on the unique sand; you must stay on wooden platforms or sun loungers).
Logistics: Requires a car/bus trip plus a boat trip.

Who is Cleopatra Beach suitable for?
This beach is a must-visit for everyone vacationing in Marmaris. It will appeal to lovers of history, beautiful photos, and unique natural phenomena. It is an ideal trip for a whole day to see a completely different, "tropical" Turkey and touch its ancient secrets.
