# Sedir Island (Sedir Adası) — Cleopatra Beach and the Ruins of Ancient Cedrae

Sedir Island (Cleopatra Island) in the Gulf of Gökova is famous for its unique organic oolite sand and the ancient ruins of the historical city of Cedrae.

This protected natural and archaeological site is legendary for its crystal-clear turquoise waters and the myth that Mark Antony shipped the pearl-like sand here from Egypt for Cleopatra.

### Pearl Sand and Ancient Cedrae
The primary attraction of Sedir Island is Cleopatra Beach. Its golden sand consists of tiny, spherical oolitic grains that resemble miniature pearls. This unique sand does not heat up under the sun and keeps the water perfectly transparent. Because of its rarity, the sand is strictly protected: visitors are not allowed to walk on it with shoes, lay towels directly on it, or take any sand from the beach (showers are installed at the exit to wash off any clinging grains).

Beyond the beach, the island hosts the ruins of the ancient Carian city of Cedrae. Wooden pathways lead visitors through the archaeological park to a well-preserved ancient theater with sea views, the remnants of Hellenistic city walls, an agora, the Temple of Apollo, and a Byzantine basilica.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** Gulf of Gökova, about 23 km north of [Marmaris](/en/marmaris), near the village of Çamlı
- **Road:** Boat access only. You can drive via paved road to Çamlı Pier (Çamlı İskelesi) and take a regular shuttle boat (about a 20-minute ride). Alternatively, join a daily boat tour departing from Marmaris or Akyaka.
- **Infrastructure:** Restrooms, changing rooms, showers, and a state-run cafe are available on the island. Sunbeds and umbrellas are set up on wooden platforms adjacent to the protected sandy beach.
- **Entrance:** Paid. The admission fee to the archaeological site is approximately €13 (free for Museum Pass Türkiye holders). The boat transfer from Çamlı is paid separately.
- **Tip:** When booking a daily boat tour from Marmaris, always check if the island entrance fee is included in the price. Arrive early in the morning around 9:00 AM to enjoy the beach and ruins before the crowds of daily tour boats arrive.
