# Transport: How to Get to Datça and Move Around the Peninsula

**The Datça Peninsula is a rather secluded place, and this remoteness is exactly what has helped it preserve its unique atmosphere. However, getting here and moving around the peninsula is not as difficult as it might seem.**

---

### How to Get to Datça

**By Plane:**
The closest airports to Datça are:
1.  **Dalaman Airport (DLM):** Located about 160 km away (a 2.5–3 hour drive). This is the most popular option.
2.  **Milas-Bodrum Airport (BJV):** Located about 170 km away (including the ferry).

From both airports, Datça can be reached in several ways:
-   **Car Rental:** The most convenient way, providing complete freedom of movement across the entire peninsula. Rental company desks are available at both airports.
-   **Transfer:** You can book a private or shared transfer in advance. This is a comfortable but more expensive option.
-   **Public Transport:** The most budget-friendly but longest route. From Dalaman Airport, you must first take the Havaş bus to the Marmaris bus station, and then transfer to a local minibus (dolmuş) to Datça.

**By Ferry from Bodrum:**
During the summer season (usually from May to October), a car ferry operates between Bodrum and Datça.
-   **Travel Time:** About 1.5–2 hours.
-   **Benefits:** This is a very scenic and convenient way to get to Datça if you fly into Bodrum or are traveling along the Aegean coast. The ferry arrives at Körmen Port (Körmen Limanı), which is about 10 km from the center of Datça.

**By Bus:**
The Datça Bus Station (Datça Otogar) can be reached by intercity buses from major Turkish cities such as Istanbul, Ankara, and Izmir. From nearby major resorts like Marmaris and Fethiye, regular minibuses (dolmuşes) run to Datça.

---

### Moving Around the Peninsula

-   **Rented Car or Scooter:** The ideal option for exploring the numerous bays, beaches, and villages of the peninsula. The roads are mostly good, but often narrow and winding.
-   **Dolmuşes (Minibuses):** The minibus network is well-developed. From the central Datça bus station, you can reach most popular destinations:
    -   To the villages of Palamutbükü and Mesudiye (Ovabükü, Hayıtbükü).
    -   To Old Datça (Eski Datça).
    -   To the ruins of Knidos (during the summer season).
    -   To Körmen Port, timed with the arrival/departure of the Bodrum ferry.
-   **Taxi:** Available, but it is the most expensive way to get around. Best for short trips.
-   **Boat Tours:** For visiting the most secluded bays inaccessible by land, the best transport is a boat or yacht as part of a one-day sea tour.
