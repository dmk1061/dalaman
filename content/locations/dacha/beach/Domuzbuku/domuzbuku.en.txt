Domuzbükü ("Pig Bay") — A legendary refuge for wild boars and free spirits
On the "other" side of the Datça Peninsula, away from the popular southern bays, lies hidden Domuzbükü. It got its straightforward name ("Pig Bay" or "Boar Ravine") for a reason: wild boars (domuz) living in the local forests indeed often come down to the stream in the bay to drink. But for travelers, this place has become synonymous not with boars, but with absolute freedom and disconnection from civilization.

Main feature: Pristine nature and access only by sea
Like some other wild bays in the region, Domuzbükü is practically inaccessible by land. There are no roads leading to it, only difficult and long trekking paths. Therefore, the only real way to get here is by water. This bay is a must-stop for many "Blue Cruises" (Mavi Tur) and private yachts sailing along the coast of the Gulf of Gökova. It is thanks to this isolation that it has preserved its pristine, almost untouched beauty.

The legend of the bay: A cabin without civilization
For many years, the symbol of Domuzbükü was a single cabin-restaurant on the shore, run by a family—Salih and his wife. It was a legendary place without electricity, mobile connection, or any amenities. Yacht travelers would stop here to eat simple but incredibly delicious gözleme (Turkish flatbreads), drink tea, and chat around the campfire. This place was the embodiment of "digital detox" long before it became fashionable. Although the situation with this establishment may have changed now, the spirit of this simple, heartfelt hospitality has forever remained part of the legend of Domuzbükü.

Beach and atmosphere
Coastline: The beach consists of large pebbles, the water is crystal clear and very calm, as the bay is well-sheltered.

Atmosphere: Peaceful, meditative, slightly hippie. This is a place for contemplation, reading books, and long conversations. There is nothing here to remind you of the hustle and bustle of the modern world. Only you, the sea, the mountains, and perhaps some wild boar tracks on the sand.

Pros and Cons
Pros:
- Complete isolation from civilization.
- A unique experience of authentic, wild recreation.
- Stunning untouched nature and the cleanest sea.
- Status of a legendary spot among Turkish sailors.

Cons:
- No access by land.
- Complete lack of amenities and infrastructure.
- Suitable only for a specific type of traveler.

Who is Domuzbükü for?
This bay is for true romantics and adventurers. For yachtsmen, sea cruise passengers, wild camping enthusiasts, and anyone looking for not just a vacation, but a real adventure and a chance to "get lost" in order to find themselves. If you want to see one of the most authentic and non-touristy sides of Datça, and you are not afraid of the lack of comfort, Domuzbükü should be on your list.
