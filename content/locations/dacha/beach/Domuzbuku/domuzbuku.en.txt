# Domuzbuku Bay (Domuzbükü) — Wild Boar Cove for Secluded Getaways

Domuzbuku is a secluded, wild bay on the Datça Peninsula featuring crystal clear waters, bordered by pine forests and accessible only by sea.

Its literal name ("Pig Bay" or "Boar Ravine") comes from the wild boars (domuz) that occasionally descend from the surrounding forests to drink at the local stream. However, for travelers, this cove is synonymous with absolute freedom and a digital detox.

Reaching Domuzbuku by land is very difficult, as there are no roads, only challenging sections of the Carian Trail. The only practical way to visit is by water. The bay is a popular anchorage for yachts and Blue Cruises (Mavi Tur) sailing the [Datça](/en/dacha) coast. For years, the bay's icon was a rustic cabin run by Salih, serving wood-fired gözleme, but today the beach remains entirely wild. The shoreline is made of large pebbles, and the sea is calm and exceptionally clean.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** Datça Peninsula, Gulf of Gökova, about 12 km by water from [Datça](/en/dacha) harbor
- **Road:** Boat only (excursion boats, yachts) or on foot via challenging Carian Trail routes
- **Infrastructure:** None (wild pebbly beach, no toilets, showers, or shops)
- **Entrance:** Free
- **Tip:** Make sure to bring your own water, food, and sun protection. Water shoes are highly recommended due to the large pebbles along the coast.
