Palamutbükü — Endless turquoise in the embrace of almond orchards
Palamutbükü is not just a bay, but an entire valley and a village of the same name, located about 25 km from the town of Datça. This is a place of a different scale and a different atmosphere: more spacious, vast, and imbued with the spirit of rural freedom. While the beaches of Datça are cozy town bays, Palamutbükü is a majestic coastal valley with one of the longest beaches on the entire peninsula.

Feature: Space and natural harmony
The main thing that strikes you in Palamutbükü is its length. The long, wide shoreline, stretching for several kilometers, guarantees that it is never crowded here, even in the very peak of the season. The beach is framed by a fertile valley famous for its almond and olive groves. This harmony of the turquoise sea and green gardens creates a unique sense of peace and unity with nature.

Beach and atmosphere
Coastline: The beach here is mixed—mostly small and medium pebbles, but there are also sandy sections. The water is exceptionally clean and transparent, and its color varies from light blue near the shore to deep sapphire at depth.

Atmosphere: Palamutbükü has a relaxed, slightly bohemian atmosphere. This place is popular among Turkish intellectuals, artists, and writers who value it for its simplicity, beauty, and tranquility. There is no hustle and bustle here, life flows measuredly and slowly.

Infrastructure: A sequence of family-run guesthouses, small boutique hotels, cafes, and restaurants stretches along the entire waterfront. Many restaurants are famous for their home-style cooking, fresh fish (the name "Palamut" means "bonito"), and dishes prepared from local farm products.

What to do
Swimming: Enjoy the cleanest water and space.

Walks: Take long walks or runs along the endless beach.

Gastronomy: Explore local restaurants, trying fresh fish and dishes made with olives and almonds of local production.

Exploring the surroundings: Rent a bicycle and ride around the village and almond groves.

Pros and Cons
Pros:
- Very long and spacious beach.
- Exceptional clarity and transparency of the water.
- Beautiful natural landscapes (sea, mountains, gardens).
- Quiet, authentic, and relaxed atmosphere.
- Excellent local cuisine.

Cons:
- Remoteness from Datça town: Transport is required to get here.
- Predominantly pebble beach, which not everyone likes.
- Can be windy, as the bay is more open compared to others.

Who is Palamutbükü for?
Palamutbükü is the ideal place for those seeking a complete "switch-off" and immersion in nature. It is suitable for a long, quiet holiday, for families, for romantic couples, and for everyone tired of the crowdedness and noise of big resorts. If you want to wake up to the singing of birds, have breakfast in the garden, spend days on an endless beach with crystal clear water, and dine on fresh fish in a simple family restaurant, Palamutbükü will become a paradise on earth for you.
