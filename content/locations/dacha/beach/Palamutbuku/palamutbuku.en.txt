# Palamutbükü Beach (Palamutbükü) — Expansive Turquoise Sea and Scenic Orchards

Palamutbükü Beach is a vast pebble shoreline on the Datça Peninsula, famous for its exceptionally clear turquoise water and surrounding almond orchards.

This long coastal strip is widely considered one of the most beautiful and popular destinations on the peninsula. Stretching for nearly two kilometers, the wide beach ensures there is plenty of space for visitors, even during the peak summer months.

### Beach Features and Amenities
The shore is covered in flat, smooth pebbles of small to medium size, mixed with occasional sandy patches. Due to the pebbly bottom, the sea remains crystal clear, offering excellent visibility that makes it highly popular for snorkeling. The water exhibits a striking turquoise hue, and the descent into the sea is relatively gradual, though water shoes are highly recommended.

The promenade running along the beach is lined with a variety of accommodation options, including cozy apart-hotels and local pensions. There are numerous beachfront cafes and fish restaurants. The bay is named after Atlantic bonito (palamut in Turkish), which you can enjoy fresh at the local seaside taverns.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** [Datça](/en/dacha) Peninsula, about 25–30 km southwest of the town center
- **Road:** Asphalt (a scenic route with tight curves). Regular minibuses (dolmuş) run from the center of Datça in summer.
- **Infrastructure:** Cafes, fish taverns, changing cabins, showers, toilets, sunbed and umbrella rentals
- **Entrance:** Free (sunbed rental is paid or included with food and drink purchases at beachfront restaurants)
- **Tip:** Be sure to bring water shoes for an easy entry into the sea and a snorkeling mask to fully enjoy the incredibly clear water.
