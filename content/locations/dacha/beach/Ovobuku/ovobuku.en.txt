# Ovabükü Beach (Ovabükü) — A Secluded Haven for Peaceful Relaxation

Ovabükü Beach is a tranquil sand-and-pebble bay on the Datça Peninsula, renowned for its untouched nature, serene atmosphere, and peaceful rural setting.

Unlike the busier neighboring Palamutbükü, this wide beach is surrounded by lush green hills, olive groves, and local orchards. It is the perfect destination for travelers seeking solitude, digital detox, and a break from the crowds.

### Nature and Vibe
The shoreline at Ovabükü is relatively wide and long, composed of a mix of sand and smooth pebbles. The water is incredibly clear, showing a beautiful gradient from turquoise to deep blue. As the sea entry can be stony in some areas, water shoes are recommended for a comfortable swim.

There are no large, noisy resorts or nightclubs here. The local infrastructure consists of family-run pensions, quiet campsites, and cozy local taverns (lokantas) serving Aegean dishes made from fresh local ingredients. From Ovabükü, you can easily walk to the adjacent smaller coves of Hayıtbükü and Kızılbük.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** [Datça](/en/dacha) Peninsula, approximately 15–20 km southwest of the town center
- **Road:** Asphalt (winding mountain roads). Regular minibuses (dolmuş) operate from Datça center during the summer season.
- **Infrastructure:** Family-run cafes and restaurants, changing cabins, showers, toilets, sunbed and umbrella rentals
- **Entrance:** Free (sunbed rental is paid or included with a cafe purchase)
- **Tip:** Bring water shoes due to pebbles at the water's edge. The bay is ideal for yoga, reading, and enjoying nature in complete tranquility.
