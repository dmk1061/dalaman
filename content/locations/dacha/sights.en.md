# Datça Sights

**Datça is not only about the sea and beaches, but also possesses a rich cultural and historical heritage. The main points of attraction for curious travelers are the majestic ruins of the ancient city of Knidos and the charming village of Eski Datça (Old Datça).**

---

### [🏛️ <u>**Knidos**</u>](/dacha/sight/knidos)

The ancient city of Knidos, located at the very tip of the peninsula where the Aegean and Mediterranean seas meet, is the main historical attraction of the region.

-   **History:** In antiquity, Knidos was a thriving Dorian city, famous for its achievements in science, art, and architecture. It was home to the famous statue of Aphrodite of Knidos—the first full-scale sculpture of a nude woman in history, which attracted pilgrims from all over the ancient world.
-   **What to see:**
    -   **Two theaters:** One smaller theater overlooking the Aegean Sea, and a second, large amphitheater facing the Mediterranean harbor.
    -   **Ruins of the Temple of Aphrodite:** The round foundation of the temple where the legendary statue once stood.
    -   **Agora and colonnaded street:** The heart of the ancient city.
    -   **Byzantine church** with well-preserved mosaic floors.
-   **Tip:** Come to Knidos closer to evening to catch the sunset. The sun setting behind the ancient ruins against the backdrop of the two seas is an unforgettable sight.

---

### [🏘️ <u>**Eski Datça (Old Datça)**</u>](/dacha/sight/eskidacha)

Unlike the port center, Old Datça is located a few kilometers inland and is an oasis of peace and authenticity.

-   **Atmosphere:** This is a beautifully preserved village with cobblestone streets and stone houses covered with bright bougainvillea flowers. Many old houses have been carefully restored and turned into small boutique hotels, art galleries, craft workshops, and cozy cafes.
-   **What to do:**
    -   **Stroll aimlessly:** The best way to experience Old Datça is simply to get lost in its streets, looking at the details and enjoying the quiet.
    -   **Can Yücel House-Museum:** Visit the house where one of the most beloved Turkish poets of the 20th century, Can Yücel, lived and worked. His poetry is inextricably linked to his love for Datça.
    -   **Have a coffee:** Relax in one of the many cafes in the shade of olive trees.

---

### Other Interesting Places

-   [⚓ <u>**Datça Port**</u>](/dacha/sight/port): Walk along the "Sevgi Yolu" (Love Path) promenade, look at the fishing boats and yachts.
-   [🛍️ <u>**Saturday Market**</u>](/dacha/sight/smarket): If you find yourself in Datça on a Saturday, be sure to visit the local market where farmers sell fresh vegetables, fruits, olives, almonds, honey, and the famous local herbs.
-   [💨 <u>**Windmills**</u>](/dacha/sight/mills): On the hills around Datça, you can see old stone windmills, which are a symbol of the region.
