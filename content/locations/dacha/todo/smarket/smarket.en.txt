# Datça Saturday Market (Cumartesi Pazarı) — A Colorful Aegean Bazaar

The Datça Saturday Market is a bustling weekly bazaar in the town center, offering fresh local produce, olive oil, almonds, herbs, and Turkish textiles.

Held at the town's covered marketplace (Pazaryeri), this vibrant event is where locals do their weekly grocery shopping and visitors experience authentic Turkish market culture.

### What to Buy and Taste
The bazaar is split into two sections: food and dry goods. The food section features fresh seasonal fruits and vegetables from surrounding villages, the famous Datça almonds (Datça bademi), cold-pressed olive oil, thyme and pine honey, homemade cheeses, and marinated olives. Make sure to try freshly baked gözleme (thin Turkish flatbreads) prepared on hot iron griddles by local women.

The textile and clothing section offers high-quality Turkish cotton towels, bed linens, clothing, and traditional hammam towels (peştemal) at budget-friendly prices. Vendors are friendly, and sampling food before buying is common practice.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** Central [Datça](/en/dacha), İskele Mahallesi, covered market square (Pazaryeri)
- **Road:** Asphalt (within easy walking distance from the town center and bus terminal)
- **Infrastructure:** Covered stalls, public restrooms, nearby parking, street food stalls
- **Entrance:** Free
- **Tip:** Arrive early (between 9:00 and 11:00 AM) to get the best produce and avoid the midday heat and crowds. Bring cash (Turkish Lira), as credit cards are rarely accepted. Finding nearby parking can be challenging in the summer.
