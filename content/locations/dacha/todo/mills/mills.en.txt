Kizlan Windmills — The Silent Guardians of Datca
The wind mills in the village of Kizlan are one of the main landmarks and most photogenic attractions of the Datca Peninsula. Built in the 19th century, these historic stone windmills are picturesquely lined up on a hill, catching the winds blowing from the sea. Of the six surviving mills, one has been restored and converted into a boutique restaurant, while the others serve as a beautiful backdrop for walks and photo shoots.

History and Significance
The mills were built by locals to grind flour, taking advantage of the strong winds blowing across the isthmus of the Datca Peninsula. They operated for many decades, providing the region with bread. The strict cylindrical stone towers with conical wooden roofs and long wooden blades are classic examples of Aegean architecture. Over time, with the arrival of electricity and modern technology, the mills were abandoned, but today they are recognized as cultural heritage sites and carefully protected.

What to Do
Walks and photo shoots: This is one of the most popular spots for wedding and tourist photo shoots, especially at sunset, when the setting sun paints the white walls of the mills in warm orange tones.
Visit the restaurant: A restaurant is open in the first mill, where you can dine in a unique historical atmosphere.
Shopping for souvenirs: Local olive oil, almonds, and honey — Datca's main treasures — are often sold nearby.

Pros and Cons
Pros:
Very beautiful and photogenic historical site.
Easy to get to, located right off the main road at the entrance to Datca.
Free access for exploring and walking around outside.
Cons:
You cannot go inside most of the mills.
Exploring the site takes no more than 20-30 minutes.

Who Will Like It?
The Kizlan Windmills will appeal to lovers of history, beautiful architecture, and photography. It is an excellent short stop on the way to the peninsula, which immediately sets a romantic and relaxed mood for Datca.
