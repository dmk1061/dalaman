# Kızlan Windmills (Kızlan Yel Değirmenleri) — Historic Guardians of Datça

The Kızlan Windmills are a row of historic 19th-century stone mills on a hill near the entrance to the Datça Peninsula, serving as a scenic landmark.

These picturesque structures were built by locals to grind flour, utilizing the strong winds blowing across the peninsula's narrow neck. Today, they stand as a classic example of traditional Aegean architecture.

### History and Current Status
A total of six cylindrical stone mills with conical roofs align on the hill in Kızlan village. After they ceased operation, the windmills were designated as cultural heritage sites.

Currently, the mills are in varying states of use: the first mill has been fully restored and operates as a traditional restaurant, the second is used as a private residence, and the remaining four are preserved for outdoor viewing. The hill surrounding the mills is one of the most popular photo locations on the peninsula. During the tourist season, local farmers often sell olive oil, honey, and almonds nearby.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** [Datça](/en/dacha) Peninsula, Kızlan village, about 8–10 km northeast of Datça town center
- **Road:** Asphalt highway (Marmaris-Datça road), followed by a 200-meter turnoff. Easily accessible by car or any minibus (dolmuş) heading toward Marmaris.
- **Infrastructure:** Restaurant inside one mill, nearby cafes, roadside parking
- **Entrance:** Free (outdoor exploration is open to the public at any time)
- **Tip:** A visit here takes only about 20–30 minutes, making it a convenient stop on your way to Datça or Marmaris. Plan your visit for sunset, when the golden hour light makes the white stone walls incredibly photogenic.
