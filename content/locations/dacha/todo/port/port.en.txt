# Datça Harbor (Datça Limanı) — The Vibrant Heart of the Town's Marina Life

Datça Harbor is a picturesque city marina and the hub of local evening life, connecting two main town beaches: the sandy Kumluk and the pebbly Taşlık.

This elegant marina serves as both a mooring site for excursion boats and private yachts, and a central promenade where the town’s tourist activity is focused.

### Atmosphere and Activities at the Harbor
During the day, the harbor has a relaxed working vibe: captains prep their boats for boat tours, and local fishermen tend to their nets. Shade-covered cafes line the waterfront, offering the perfect spot to enjoy a glass of Turkish tea or a cup of coffee while watching the quiet bay.

At night, the harbor transforms into a lively social hub. The promenade fills with locals and tourists, street musicians perform, and waterfront bars and restaurants come alive, serving fresh catch-of-the-day fish and traditional mezes. This is also the starting point for daily boat trips around the peninsula's hidden coves. Look out for the bronze statue of Badem, a Mediterranean monk seal, which stands as a symbol of the harbor.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** Central [Datça](/en/dacha), the main city harbor
- **Road:** Asphalt (easily reachable on foot from anywhere in the town center)
- **Infrastructure:** Yacht docks, walking promenade, numerous cafes, fish restaurants, bars, souvenir shops, and restrooms in local venues
- **Entrance:** Free for pedestrians (yacht mooring and services are paid based on vessel size)
- **Tip:** Visit the harbor around sunset. The views of the sun dipping behind the hills are spectacular, and it’s the best time to select a restaurant for dining by the water.
