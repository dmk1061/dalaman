Eski Datça — A stone fairy tale drowning in bougainvillea flowers
If modern Datça is about sea breeze and the noise of the promenade, then Eski Datça is the soul of the peninsula, hidden in the shade of almond trees. This is a place where time has stopped. Once abandoned, today this village has turned into a meticulously restored architectural reserve and a bohemian paradise.

Main feature: Stone houses and bougainvillea
The trademark of Eski Datça is its visual harmony. Narrow, cobblestone streets wind between old stone houses with tiled roofs and bright wooden shutters. But the main color of Eski Datça is dazzling pink, violet, and purple. Lush thickets of bougainvillea wrap around almost every house, cascading from the walls and creating incredibly photogenic arches over the streets.

The spirit of the poet: Can Yücel
It is impossible to talk about Eski Datça without mentioning Can Yücel—one of the most beloved and famous poets of Turkey. He spent the last years of his life here, and his spirit still defines the atmosphere of this place. His simple house can be found on one of the streets, and his favorite cafe, where he spent time chatting, has become a place of pilgrimage. It is thanks to him that Eski Datça acquired a reputation as a refuge for artists, intellectuals, and everyone who values freedom and the simple beauty of life.

Atmosphere and what to do
Atmosphere: Quiet, peaceful, creative, and incredibly romantic.

What to do: The main activity here is to wander aimlessly through the streets, admiring every detail. Visit tiny workshops and galleries where local craftsmen sell unique products made of ceramics, glass, and silver. Sit in one of the cozy cafes located in shady inner courtyards and drink coffee or homemade lemonade.

Pros and Cons
Pros:
- Incredibly photogenic and beautiful place.
- Unique romantic and creative atmosphere.
- Many unique shops with handmade products.
- Ideal place for unhurried walks and relaxing in cafes.

Cons:
- The village is quite small; you can walk around it in an hour or two.
- In the peak of the season, the main street can be quite crowded.
- This place is more about atmosphere than specific "sights."

Who is Eski Datça for?
Eski Datça is a must-visit for everyone vacationing on the peninsula. It will especially appeal to romantics, photographers, connoisseurs of beauty, art lovers, and those looking for unique handmade souvenirs. It is the perfect place to spend a few hours, take a break from the beach heat, and immerse yourself in a completely different, fairy-tale reality.
