# Eski Datça (Old Datça) — Stone Architecture and Bohemian Charm

Eski Datça is a historic neighborhood of stone houses, cobblestone streets, and cascading bougainvillea flowers, capturing the bohemian spirit of Datça.

Located just a few kilometers from the modern town center, this beautifully restored settlement serves as a pedestrian-friendly sanctuary of traditional Aegean architecture.

### Atmosphere, Art, and Poetry
The hallmark of Eski Datça is its winding cobblestone lanes bordered by centuries-old stone structures with colorful wooden shutters. Bougainvillea in shades of pink and purple spills over garden walls, forming picturesque archways. The neighborhood is home to artisan workshops, boutique galleries, and craft shops selling handmade ceramics, olive oil soap, and local delicacies such as pine honey and Datça almonds.

Eski Datça is deeply connected to Can Yücel, one of Turkey's most beloved contemporary poets, who spent his final years here. Visitors can walk past his historic house (which remains a private residence but can be viewed from the outside) and visit his favorite local coffeehouse, which remains a popular gathering spot for artists and writers.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** [Datça](/en/dacha) Peninsula, approximately 2.5 km north of the modern town center (Merkez)
- **Road:** Asphalt (easily reachable by local roads). Regular minibuses (dolmuş) connect Datça center to Eski Datça in the summer; it is also accessible by bicycle or on foot.
- **Infrastructure:** Boutique hotels, cafes, traditional restaurants, craft shops, parking lots at the entrance
- **Entrance:** Free (parking at the entrance may carry a small fee during the peak season)
- **Tip:** The area is small and can be explored in 1 to 2 hours. It is best visited in the late afternoon when the temperature drops, allowing for a relaxed walk and dinner in one of the hidden garden courtyards.
