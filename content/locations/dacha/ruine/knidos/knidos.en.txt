Knidos — The City of Aphrodite at the edge of the earth
On the westernmost point of the Datça Peninsula, there, where the Aegean and Mediterranean seas meet, lie the ruins of ancient Knidos. This is not just a heap of stones, but the remains of a once prosperous and influential city, which was known in the ancient world for its wealth, science, art, and, most importantly, the first nude statue of the goddess Aphrodite in history.

Main feature: A place of power and the cult of Aphrodite
Strategic position: Knidos was built on terraces sloping down to the sea and had two harbors: one for merchant ships, the other for warships. This unique location at the "edge of the earth" made it a major maritime and trading center.

Temple and statue of Aphrodite: The main sanctuary of the city was the round temple of the goddess of love Aphrodite, inside which stood her legendary statue by the great sculptor Praxiteles. This was the first full-scale sculptural depiction of a nude female body in Greek art. The statue was so famous that pilgrims from all over the ancient world flocked to Knidos just to see it. The original is lost, but the fame of Knidos is forever associated with this masterpiece.

What to see today
Two harbors: Stroll along the isthmus between the two bays and imagine how life bustled here.

Amphitheaters: There were two of them in the city. The lower one, near the port, is better preserved and impresses with its size and sea view.

Main street (Stoa): Walk along the ancient paved road where shops and public buildings once stood.

Ruins of temples: Find the foundation of the round temple of Aphrodite and try to feel the magic of this place.

Sundial: See the well-preserved marble time indicator created by the famous astronomer and mathematician Eudoxus, who lived and worked in Knidos.

Atmosphere and tips
A visit to Knidos is an immersion in history with incredible visual accompaniment. Wherever you look, your eyes will meet the azure sea.

Best time to visit: Early morning or evening, closer to sunset. During the day it can be very hot here, and there is practically no shade. Sunset in Knidos, especially with a view from the lighthouse located a little further away, is an unforgettable sight.

What to bring: Comfortable shoes, a hat, plenty of water, and a camera.

Pros and Cons
Pros:
- Huge historical and cultural significance.
- Stunning location and breathtaking scenery.
- Opportunity to touch thousands of years of history.
- Relatively uncrowded compared to other ancient giants like Ephesus.

Cons:
- Remoteness from major resorts.
- Requires physical effort, as you will have to walk a lot in hilly terrain.
- Hot in the summer noon.

Who is Knidos for?
Knidos is a must-visit for everyone interested in history, archaeology, and ancient culture. It will also capture the hearts of photographers and lovers of beautiful landscapes. It is an ideal day trip from Datça or Marmaris for those who want to diversify their beach holiday with something truly meaningful and impressive. This is the culminating point of a journey along the Datça Peninsula, leaving a deep and meaningful trace in the memory.
