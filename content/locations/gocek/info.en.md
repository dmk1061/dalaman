Göcek is a small but world-famous elite resort town in the Muğla province, located between Fethiye and Dalyan. It is cozily nestled in a large and secluded bay surrounded by pine-covered mountains, making it one of the most protected and beautiful natural harbors in the Mediterranean.

### **Capital of Yacht Tourism**

Göcek is rightfully considered the unofficial capital of yacht tourism in Turkey. The town is famous for its six world-class marinas, including D-Marin, Skopea Marina, and Marinturk, which attract yachtsmen from all over the world.
*   **Protected Harbor:** Its unique location in a bay dotted with islands and coves ensures calm waters and protection from winds, creating ideal conditions for sailing.
*   **Natural Beauty:** Göcek and its surroundings are part of a **Special Environmental Protection Area**, which limits construction and helps preserve pristine nature. The construction of multi-story buildings is prohibited here, allowing the town to maintain its cozy and harmonious appearance.

### **Atmosphere and Recreation**

Unlike large and noisy resorts, Göcek offers an atmosphere of tranquility, luxury, and privacy.
*   **Elite Resort:** The town is popular among celebrities, business people, and everyone who values quiet and high-quality relaxation. Exquisite restaurants, boutiques, and cafes are located on the promenade.
*   **Legends and History:** In ancient times, this place was known as **Kalimache**. According to local legend, it was here that Icarus, escaping from captivity, fell into the sea.

### **Things to Do**

*   **Boat Trips:** The main entertainment in Göcek is, of course, the sea. Dozens of boats and yachts depart daily from the port for excursions to the **12 Islands**.
*   **Hiking:** The picturesque hills around the town offer excellent trekking routes with panoramic views of the bay.
*   **Relaxed Holiday:** Enjoy the slow-paced atmosphere on the promenade, taste Mediterranean cuisine, and just relax away from the hustle and bustle.
