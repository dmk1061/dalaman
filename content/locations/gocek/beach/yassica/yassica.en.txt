# Yassica Islands (Yassıca Adaları) — Uninhabited Archipelago and Sandy Spits

Yassica Islands are a scenic group of flat, uninhabited islets in the Gulf of Göcek with gentle sandy spits forming a protected shallow lagoon.

The archipelago consists of five small islands located very close to each other. The main highlight of this place is a long sandy spit extending into the sea from the largest island. At the tip of this spit, a shallow natural pool is formed, which is ideal for families with children as the water is warm and calm, and the depth increases very gradually.

It is possible to wade between some of the islands on foot, and on one of them lies a small natural pond known as the "Devil's Pool". There is no permanent infrastructure on the Yassica Islands; you will not find any toilets, showers, sunbeds, or cafes. All essential amenities are provided on board the visiting yachts. However, during the summer season, local vendors on small boats often anchor nearby to sell traditional Turkish gözleme (pancakes), ice cream, and cold drinks.

The islands are accessible by water only. They are a staple stop on the daily boat tours departing from the harbors of [Göcek](/en/gocek) and [Fethiye](/en/fethiye) (such as the popular "12 Islands Tour"), as well as for private yachts and water taxis.

### Practical Information
- **Location:** Gulf of Göcek, 7 km by water from [Göcek](/en/gocek)
- **Road:** Boat only (accessible via excursion boats, private yachts, or water taxis)
- **Infrastructure:** None (wild islands, temporary food vendors on boats during the season)
- **Entrance:** Free (no entry fee to the islands, only boat transfers or tours are paid)
- **Tip:** We highly recommend wearing water shoes when walking along the sandy spit and wading between the islands, as there may be sharp stones or sea urchins on the seabed.
