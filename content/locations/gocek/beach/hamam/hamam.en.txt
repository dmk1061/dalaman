Hamam Bay — Swim in History at Cleopatra's Baths
Hamam Bay, also known as Cleopatra's Bay, is a place where natural beauty and ancient history literally submerge into one another. Surrounded by dense pine and olive forests, this bay is famous not only for its crystal-clear water but also for a unique historical monument — the ruins of a Byzantine bath, which are partially submerged under water.

Main Feature: Submerged Bath and the Legend of Cleopatra
Half-submerged ruins: Right by the shore in shallow waters, the stone walls and arches of an ancient bath can be found. You can swim right up to them, touch the ancient stones, and even swim inside what once were bath chambers. This creates an incredible feeling of swimming directly inside history.

Legend of Cleopatra: Although historically these are ruins of the Byzantine period, a beautiful legend states that these baths were built as a wedding gift for the Egyptian Queen Cleopatra. It is claimed that there was a thermal spring here, and the queen came here to take rejuvenating baths. Although the spring dried up long ago, the romantic legend lives on and gives this place a special charm.

Beach and Atmosphere
Coastline: There is a small pebble beach in the bay, but the main attraction is swimming and snorkeling around and inside the ruins.

Atmosphere: During the day, it is very lively. It is one of the main stops for dozens of tour boats from Gocek and Fethiye. Hundreds of people swim in the bay at the same time. However, in the early morning and evening when the tour boats leave, the bay turns into a quiet and magical place, especially for those traveling on a private yacht.

Infrastructure: On the shore, there is a restaurant with a jetty that mainly serves yachtsmen.

Pros and Cons
Pros:

A unique opportunity to swim among ancient ruins.
A very beautiful and picturesque bay with crystal-clear water.
An interesting legend that adds romance to the place.
An excellent spot for snorkeling.

Cons:

Accessible only by water.
Very crowded during the day due to tour boats.
Limited infrastructure on shore.

Who is Hamam Bay for?
This place is a must-visit for anyone embarking on the "12 Islands" boat trip from Gocek or Fethiye. It will appeal to history buffs as well as those looking for beautiful places to swim and take photos. The opportunity to literally touch history while swimming in "Cleopatra's Baths" is a unique experience that makes visiting this bay unforgettable.
