# Cleopatra's Bath (Hamam Koyu) — Legendary Semi-Submerged Byzantine Ruins

Cleopatra's Bath is a unique bay in Göcek with crystal clear turquoise water and sunken Byzantine ruins of ancient baths, accessible only by sea.

Also known as Hamam Bay (Hamam Koyu), this picturesque cove is bordered by dense pine forests. According to local legend, these baths were built specifically for the Egyptian Queen Cleopatra during her journey across the Mediterranean. Today, visitors can snorkel directly among the ancient stone walls and arches that have slipped beneath the water.

On the shoreline, rustic restaurants (such as Adaia) with wooden jetties serve private yachts and daily excursion boats touring the gulf of [Göcek](/en/gocek). There is no direct road access. You can reach the bay either by water or on foot via a mountainous section of the Lycian Way trail from [Sarsala Cove](/en/dalaman/beach/Sarsala) (about 1.5 to 2 hours hiking one way).

### Practical Information
- **Location:** Gulf of Göcek, 12 km by water from [Göcek](/en/gocek) harbor
- **Road:** Boat only (tour boats, yachts, water taxis) or on foot via a rocky Lycian Way trail (approx. 1.5–2 hours)
- **Infrastructure:** Small private jetties, shoreline restaurants, restrooms at the restaurants, wild beach
- **Entrance:** Free
- **Tip:** Wear water shoes while swimming near the ruins, as the underwater stones can be sharp and slippery. Sturdy trekking boots and a good supply of water are essential if you hike the trail from [Sarsala](/en/dalaman/beach/Sarsala).
