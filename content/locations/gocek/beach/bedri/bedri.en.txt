# Bedri Rahmi Bay (Bedri Rahmi Koyu) — Artistic Symbol of Gocek Gulf

Bedri Rahmi Bay is a scenic cove in Gocek accessible only by boat, famous for a unique fish painting on a rock and ancient Lycian rock tombs on the hill.

### History and Art
Historically known as Tasyaka Bay (Taşyaka), it was renamed in honor of the prominent Turkish painter and poet, Bedri Rahmi Eyuboglu. During one of the first "Blue Cruises" in 1974, he painted a fish on a large rock by the shore, inside of which six other fish are secretly depicted. This painting has since become the iconic symbol of yachting cruises in the [Gocek](/en/gocek) area.

### Nature and Dining
The bay is framed by pine forests and olive trees. The beach is pebbly, and the turquoise waters are calm and exceptionally clear. On the hills behind the bay, ancient Lycian rock tombs are visible. The bay has evolved to feature high-end dining concepts (like ONNO Grill & Bar and Miori by the Sea) with wooden docks for yachts, toilets, and showers for diners. Direct road access does not exist.

### Practical Information
* **Location:** Gocek Gulf, 10 km (5.5 nautical miles) by water from [Gocek](/en/gocek) harbor.
* **Road:** Boat only (excursion boats, private yachts, water taxis).
* **Infrastructure:** Wooden piers, upscale restaurants, restrooms and showers at restaurants, wild pebble beach.
* **Entrance:** Free (you only pay for transport or boat rental).
* **Tip:** Wear sturdy closed shoes (sneakers) to hike up to the Lycian tombs. Bring water shoes due to the pebbly shore and a snorkeling mask to enjoy the crystal-clear water.
