Bedri Rahmi Bay — Where Art Meets the Sea
Bedri Rahmi Bay, whose real name is Taşyaka, is not just a beautiful bay, but a place where one of the greatest figures of Turkish art left his mark. Surrounded by pine and olive trees, this bay is famous not only for its natural beauty, but also for a unique work of art that has become its symbol.

Main Feature: The Fish on the Rock
In 1974, the famous Turkish artist, writer, and poet Bedri Rahmi Eyüboğlu stopped in this bay during a "blue cruise" with his friends. Inspired by its beauty, he found a large flat stone by the shore and painted a fish on it in his signature style. But this is not just a fish. If you look closely, six small fish are hidden inside this big fish, which are said to symbolize his six companions on that journey.

This simple but meaningful drawing changed the fate of the bay forever. Over time, its original name Taşyaka was forgotten, and everyone began to call it Bedri Rahmi Bay. This drawing became a symbol of "blue cruises" and the fusion of art with nature.

Beach and Atmosphere
Coast: There is a small pebble beach in the bay, as well as a wooden pier for mooring boats. The water here is crystal clear and calm, of an emerald color.

Atmosphere: Very quiet, peaceful, and artistic. People come here not only to swim, but also to see the famous drawing, take a photo with it, and feel the spirit of this place.

Infrastructure: There is a small restaurant on the shore serving visitors to the bay. Paths go deep into the island, along which you can take a short walk among olive trees and see hidden Lycian tombs.

Pros and Cons
Pros:

A unique combination of art and nature.

Very beautiful and quiet bay with crystal clear water.

An interesting history associated with a famous artist.

A mandatory stop on the "12 Islands" tour.

Cons:

Accessible only by water.

It can be crowded during the day due to excursion boats.

Who is Bedri Rahmi Koyu suitable for?
This bay will appeal to absolutely everyone, but it will be especially appreciated by lovers of art, history, and those looking for something more than just beautiful landscapes in their travels. This is a place with a soul, where you can swim in the same waters that inspired the great artist and touch his legacy left right on the rock at the water's edge.
