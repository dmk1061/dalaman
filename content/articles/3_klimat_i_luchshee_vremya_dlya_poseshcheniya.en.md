**Welcome to our detailed guide on climate and seasons along the Turquoise Coast. The [Dalaman](/en/dalaman) region (`DLM`) and surrounding coastal gems offer exceptional experiences, rich cultural heritage, and modern travel amenities.**

## Summer: Hot, Lively, and Perfect Sea Temperatures

Summer along the turquoise coast brings peak Mediterranean warmth, vibrant beach atmosphere, and ideal swimming conditions. Daytime air temperatures regularly reach 30–36°C (86–97°F) with virtually zero rainfall between mid-June and late August. Sea water temperatures peak around 27–28°C, providing a wonderfully refreshing escape.

Key coastal destinations like [Fethiye](/en/fethiye)'s Oludeniz Blue Lagoon, [Marmaris](/en/marmaris) bay, and [Kaş](/en/kas) diving sites are bustling with travelers. If you prefer milder daytime heat, early June or late August offer a more relaxed pace with gentle sea breezes and pleasant evening temperatures ideal for waterfront dining.

## Autumn: The Golden Season for Adventures and Comfort

September and October are widely celebrated as the 'Golden Season' along the Lycian coast. As the intense summer sun softens, daytime air temperatures hover comfortably between 26–30°C (79–86°F), while the Aegean and Mediterranean waters remain thoroughly warmed from summer, often retaining a delightful 25–26°C through mid-October.

This is universally recognized as the best time for combining relaxation on pristine beaches with active exploration of ancient ruins such as Knidos in [Datça](/en/dacha) or the Lycian rock tombs in [Dalyan](/en/dalyan). Crowds thin considerably, accommodation rates become more advantageous, and local boat excursions operate in a serene, peaceful environment.

## Explore: Весна: пробуждение природы и тишина

Spring (April to May) transforms the entire Dalaman coast into a lush, blossoming paradise. Wild almond and citrus groves bloom, the air smells of pine and sea breeze, and temperatures gradually climb from 20°C in April to a balmy 26°C by late May. While sea temperatures are invigorating early in the season (around 19–21°C), May brings excellent swimming conditions.

Spring is the premier window for hiking world-famous trekking routes like the Lycian Way, kayaking around [Göcek](/en/gocek) islands, or exploring the peaceful lakeside setting of [Köyceğiz](/en/koycegiz). Nature lovers will appreciate the blooming flora and active birdlife across coastal estuaries.

## Explore: Зима: тихий сезон для культурного отдыха

Winter (December to March) offers a tranquil, authentic perspective of the Turkish Mediterranean. With mild daytime temperatures averaging 14–18°C (57–64°F) and occasional refreshing rains, coastal towns return to their peaceful local rhythms.

This is a wonderful season for cultural immersion, photography, visiting thermal spas in [Köyceğiz](/en/koycegiz), or sampling authentic Aegean culinary traditions in [Fethiye](/en/fethiye) without tourist crowds. Many expats and long-term travelers choose the winter months for quiet coastal living and refreshing walks along empty marinas.
